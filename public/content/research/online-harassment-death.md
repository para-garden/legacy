## The Mikayla Raines Case (2025)

Mikayla Raines, 29, founded SaveAFox in 2017 at age 20. By the time of her death on June 23, 2025, the nonprofit had rescued over 150 foxes. She was autistic, had borderline personality disorder, and was described by her husband Ethan as someone who "could never tell what was going on in her head" from the outside.

The harassment was sustained and came from people she knew — former friends, collaborators, community members who had moved to dedicated snark spaces. A Reddit snark page was identified as a significant vector. After her death, a petition circulated to have the page removed. Platform action was not immediate.

Ethan Raines performed CPR on her for fifteen minutes until paramedics arrived.

He said: *"She felt as if the entire world had turned against her."*

He also said: *"I do hope there's accountability."*

The snark page remained up through the initial coverage period.

## The Mechanism

**Snark communities** are dedicated spaces — subreddits, Discord servers, forum threads — organized around mocking specific creators. They operate at scale. A dedicated snark page for a mid-sized creator can accumulate thousands of members, generating a continuous feed of criticism, rumor, and personal attack directed at one person.

The target is aware the space exists. They often read it. The harassment doesn't require direct contact — ambient knowledge that a space exists where people systematically mock you is sufficient for psychological harm.

**The former-friend vector** is documented in the Raines case and recurs across similar incidents: the most damaging content often comes from people with inside knowledge — former collaborators, ex-friends, ex-moderators. They have credibility within the snark community and specific, verifiable detail that makes their contributions land harder.

**Platform accountability gap:** Snark communities typically do not violate terms of service as written. Mockery, criticism, rumor — these are legal speech. The platform's review threshold is not calibrated to cumulative psychological effect, only to individual post-level violations. A community can exist for years, publishing thousands of posts targeting one person, without a single post meeting the removal threshold.

After the target dies: the petition, the coverage, the belated review. Sometimes removal, sometimes not. No consequence for participants. No mechanism for accountability that Ethan Raines was asking for.

**BPD as a documented vulnerability factor:** Borderline personality disorder involves intense emotional responses to perceived rejection and abandonment. The harassment pattern — sustained, from people who knew her, framed as exposure of genuine wrongdoing — is precisely calibrated (not intentionally, but effectively) to the specific vulnerabilities of someone with BPD. The platform does not distinguish. The snark community does not distinguish. The result is a feedback loop where the target's responses to harassment are used as further evidence against them.

## Broader Pattern

Raines is not an isolated case. The pattern — creator builds something real, community fractures, former insiders migrate to snark spaces, sustained harassment escalates, mental health crisis, death — has documented precedents. What is consistent:

- The harassment is relational, not anonymous. Former friends are the sharpest instruments.
- The platform's inaction is not negligence in the legal sense. The content doesn't qualify.
- The accountability the surviving family asks for does not arrive.
- The snark community disperses or migrates after the death. Some members express regret. Most don't.
- The creator's work — the foxes, the rescues, the content — continues, carried by whoever remains.

What gets left: the gap between what a person built and what killed them. The distance between the person posting in the snark thread and the CPR for fifteen minutes. Nobody closes it.
