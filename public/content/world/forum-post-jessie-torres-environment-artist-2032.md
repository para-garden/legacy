**I GOT THE JOB I GOT THE JOB I GOT THE JOB**

<small>**jess_enviro** · 11:47 PM · 14 May 2032</small>

ok sorry for the all caps title but I literally cannot sleep and I need to tell someone who gets it

I got offered a junior environment artist position today. at an actual studio. in Austin. full time. FULL TIME. I start after graduation.

I'm shaking typing this lol my hands won't stop

ok let me back up. I'm 21, I go to Louisville (transferred from WKU when they started the game design track). I have been working toward this since I was 14 years old and played Silksong on a crappy used laptop that could barely run anything else. I put like 400 hours into that game. I used to draw the maps from memory in my notebook during class. I didn't even know environment art was a job. I just knew I wanted to build places that felt like that — where you could get lost and it was ok, where the world taught you where to go without a single tutorial popup.

my art teacher in high school told me game design was something you could actually study. before that I thought wanting to make games was like wanting to be a rock star. like, ok honey, sure. but she was serious about it and she helped me put a portfolio together for college apps.

I couldn't afford DigiPen or Full Sail or any of the big programs. state school, financial aid, worked at Subway through all of it (I literally cannot look at a cold cut combo anymore). stayed up in the computer lab until 3am most nights because that's when you could actually get on the good machines. built everything in Unreal. just environments and level layouts, nothing flashy, but I got really into lighting and spatial flow and how you can make a player look exactly where you want them to look without them knowing you did it.

did an unpaid QA internship at the studio last summer. I was supposed to be testing but I kept staying late to mess around with their level design tools. one of the senior artists noticed and let me build a test environment on my own time. he told me it was good. not in the "nice job sweetie" way. in the way where you can tell the person is actually looking at your work.

AND THEY OFFERED ME THE POSITION. TODAY. I am sitting on a couch that is not mine in an apartment that is not mine and I just got the email and I read it four times to make sure I wasn't misreading it.

I called my mom. she asked if they have health insurance (lol mom). and then she got quiet and said "I'm so proud of you, baby" and I could hear her telling someone in the background. probably Angie from the front desk at her work she tells Angie everything.

I know this is just a junior position. I know. but you guys. I'm going to make GAMES. for a LIVING. I'm going to build environments that people walk through and feel something. that's my job now. someone is going to pay me to do the thing I've been doing for free since I was 14.

I don't even know what to do with myself rn. I'm just sitting here in the dark grinning like an idiot.

if you're still grinding and it feels like it's never going to happen — it can happen. I'm nobody from Kentucky. I went to state school. I worked at Subway. and I got the job.

ok sorry for the novel lmao. I just needed to put this somewhere. thanks for reading if you got this far. 💜
