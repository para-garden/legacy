**Re: Grant Application #ACF-2031-WF-0847 — Community Workforce Innovation Fund**

Dear Mr. Williams,

Thank you for your application to the Arizona Community Foundation's Community Workforce Innovation Fund. We received 214 applications for this cycle and were able to fund 18 projects.

After careful review by our grants committee, we regret to inform you that your proposal for the **Phoenix Rideshare Cooperative** was not selected for funding at this time.

Your application was reviewed across our standard criteria, and we want to share the committee's feedback in the spirit of strengthening future applications:

---

### Proposal Summary (from application)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Project** | Phoenix Rideshare Cooperative — a [driver-owned platform cooperative](https://platform.coop/) providing rideshare services in the Phoenix metropolitan area |
| **Requested Amount** | $175,000 (over 18 months) |
| **Matching Funds** | $22,000 (driver buy-in commitments from 43 founding members) |
| **Proposed Use** | App development, legal incorporation as [cooperative under A.R.S. §10-2001](https://www.azleg.gov/ars/10/02001.htm), initial marketing, and driver onboarding |

**Project Description (excerpt from application):**

> "Drivers in Phoenix currently work 55–60 hours per week for Uber and Lyft and earn, after expenses, an effective hourly rate of [$6–$9/hour](https://www.epi.org/publication/uber-and-the-labor-market-uber-drivers-compensation-wages-and-the-scale-of-uber-and-the-gig-economy/) — below the federal minimum wage. Platform companies take [25–30% of each fare](https://www.sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar?action=getcompany&company=uber+technologies). Drivers have no input on pricing, no appeal process for deactivation, and no access to benefits.
>
> The Phoenix Rideshare Cooperative would be a driver-owned alternative. Drivers would pay a one-time buy-in of $500 and monthly dues of $50. The cooperative would take a flat 10% service fee — less than half the platform rate — to cover app maintenance, insurance, and administration. Pricing, policies, and deactivation decisions would be made by a driver-elected board.
>
> [Platform cooperatives exist in other markets](https://platform.coop/directory/) — [Green Taxi Cooperative in Denver](https://www.greentaxico.com/), [The Drivers Cooperative in New York City](https://www.drivers.coop/) — and have demonstrated that the model is viable. What is needed in Phoenix is seed funding to cover the fixed costs of app development and legal incorporation."

---

### Committee Feedback

**Strengths:**
- Clear identification of a real need in the Phoenix gig economy
- Strong grassroots support (43 founding member commitments)
- Alignment with the Foundation's workforce equity priorities
- Thoughtful governance structure modeled on successful cooperatives

**Concerns:**
- The application underestimates the challenge of achieving critical mass in a market dominated by Uber and Lyft. Rider acquisition costs are likely 3–5x the applicant's estimates.
- App development costs of $85,000 may be insufficient for a production-quality ride-hailing application with real-time dispatch, payment processing, and driver-rider matching.
- The cooperative model relies on sufficient driver participation to maintain reliable coverage across the Phoenix metro area. At 43 drivers, service gaps would be significant and may undermine rider confidence.
- Sustainability beyond the grant period is unclear. The 10% service fee may not cover operational costs at the projected scale.

**Recommendation:** The committee found the proposal compelling in its vision but premature in its execution plan. We encourage the applicants to consider a smaller-scale pilot (e.g., airport corridor only, or a single suburb) and to seek technical assistance from [the Platform Cooperativism Consortium](https://platform.coop/) before reapplying.

---

We encourage you to apply again in our Spring 2032 cycle. Applications open February 1 and close April 15.

Thank you for your commitment to the Phoenix community. We wish you the best in your continued work.
