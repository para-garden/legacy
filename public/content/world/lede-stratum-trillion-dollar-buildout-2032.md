**Stratum Seeks $1.2 Trillion for Global AI Compute Network**

*Infrastructure buildout would span 14 countries over six years*

---

Stratum, the AI infrastructure company, is seeking [$1.2 trillion](https://www.wsj.com/tech/ai/sam-altman-seeks-trillions-of-dollars-to-reshape-the-business-of-chips-and-ai-7a2e446b) in capital commitments for what would be the largest private infrastructure project in history, according to documents reviewed by Lede and two people familiar with the matter.

The proposed buildout, internally called Project Meridian, would construct a network of 40 to 60 high-density compute facilities across 14 countries over six years. Stratum has held preliminary discussions with sovereign wealth funds in [Abu Dhabi](https://www.reuters.com/technology/artificial-intelligence/abu-dhabis-mgx-invest-openais-new-funding-round-bloomberg-news-reports-2024-10-01/), Saudi Arabia, Singapore, and Norway, as well as with [SoftBank Group](https://www.reuters.com/technology/artificial-intelligence/trump-announce-private-sector-ai-infrastructure-investment-cbs-reports-2025-01-21/) and several large pension systems.

An initial commitment of $200 billion is being sought by the second quarter of 2033, with the remainder structured as phased capital calls tied to facility milestones. Stratum would retain operational control of the network; investors would receive capacity allocation rights and revenue participation.

Stratum CEO Nathan Coles described the raise in an internal presentation as "the final infrastructure problem."

"Compute is the bottleneck," Coles told employees in a company-wide meeting last month, according to a recording reviewed by Lede. "Everything we want to build — and everything our customers want to build — is waiting on capacity that doesn't exist yet. This [closes the gap](https://www.theverge.com/2024/2/9/24065922/sam-altman-7-trillion-ai-chip-funding) permanently."

Stratum was formed in 2029 through the [merger](https://www.reuters.com/technology/artificial-intelligence/openai-remove-non-profit-control-give-sam-altman-equity-sources-say-2024-09-25/) of two earlier AI ventures and has since raised approximately [$14 billion](https://www.reuters.com/technology/artificial-intelligence/openai-closes-66-billion-funding-haul-2024-10-02/) in equity funding. The company operates 11 compute facilities in the United States, Japan, and the United Arab Emirates. Its primary customers are enterprise AI providers, government agencies, and pharmaceutical research organizations.

Industry analysts have noted that Stratum's thesis — that [scaling compute](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/openai-google-others-find-limits-to-ai-scaling) remains the primary driver of AI capability improvement — is not universally shared. Several prominent researchers have argued that [architectural limitations](https://www.theverge.com/2024/6/24/24184451/yann-lecun-ai-agi-artificial-general-intelligence) in current model designs cannot be overcome by additional hardware alone, and that returns on compute investment have diminished since the mid-2020s.

Stratum declined to comment. Representatives for SoftBank and MGX did not respond to requests for comment.

Stratum's shares were up 2.3% in premarket trading.
