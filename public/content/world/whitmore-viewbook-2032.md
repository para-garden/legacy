## Find Your Place

**At Whitmore University, you're not a number. You're a name, a voice, a story waiting to unfold.**

For over 140 years, Whitmore has prepared students for lives of purpose, leadership, and impact. Our [15:1 student-to-faculty ratio](https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/) means you'll learn in small classes where your professors know your name — and your potential.

### Academics That Matter

Whitmore's liberal arts curriculum is designed to do more than prepare you for a career — it prepares you for a life of critical thinking, creative expression, and civic engagement.

**English & Writing** — Our nationally recognized English department offers small workshop-style classes where you'll develop your voice as a writer and thinker. First-year composition at Whitmore isn't a box to check — it's the foundation of your intellectual life.

*"My composition professor changed how I think about writing. She made me feel like my voice mattered." — Marcus T., Class of 2027*

**Sciences** — State-of-the-art labs in the Kessler Science Center. Undergraduate research opportunities beginning sophomore year. 87% of pre-med students accepted to medical school within two years of graduation.

**Business & Economics** — Our Whitmore Business Fellows program pairs juniors with regional business leaders for a year-long mentored capstone project.

---

### Campus Life

Whitmore's 285-acre campus in Whitmore, Ohio, offers the best of both worlds — a close-knit residential community just an hour from Cleveland and 40 minutes from Akron.

- **Greek life:** 6 fraternities, 5 sororities — 32% of students participate
- **Club sports and intramurals:** 40+ options
- **Student organizations:** 120+
- **Performing arts:** Theatre, orchestra, jazz ensemble, a cappella
- **Sustainability:** LEED-certified student center, campus farm, bike-share program

*"Whitmore feels like home. I found my people here." — Sofia A., Class of 2028*

**98% of Whitmore students receive financial aid.** The average financial aid package is $38,000, bringing the average net cost to approximately [$34,050 per year](https://collegescorecard.ed.gov/).

*Whitmore is committed to making a Whitmore education accessible. We meet [an average of 78% of demonstrated financial need](https://nces.ed.gov/ipeds/) through a combination of institutional grants, federal and state aid, work-study, and loans.*
