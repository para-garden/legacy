*The author posted a thought about her classroom. The platform's recommendation algorithm [identified the post's engagement velocity](https://blog.twitter.com/engineering/en_us/topics/open-source/2023/twitter-recommendation-algorithm) and distributed it to approximately 2.3 million people in 11 hours. [Social media recommendation algorithms optimize for engagement — likes, reposts, replies, and time spent — regardless of whether the engagement is positive or negative.](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2023/02/16/americans-social-media-use-2023/) Content that provokes strong reactions — agreement and disagreement — generates the highest engagement. [A 2021 study published in Science found that posts expressing moral outrage were amplified by an estimated 20% per additional moral-emotional word.](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.abe5641)*

*The author was doxxed — her name, school, and city posted publicly — within 3 hours of the post going viral. [Doxxing affects an estimated 43 million Americans.](https://www.safehome.org/resources/doxxing/) [Teachers are disproportionately targeted: a 2022 EdWeek Research Center survey found that 1 in 4 teachers reported being harassed online.](https://www.edweek.org/teaching-learning/teachers-are-being-harassed-online-heres-what-you-need-to-know/2022/04) [A 2023 Pew Research Center survey found that 46% of U.S. teens have experienced cyberbullying.](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2022/12/15/teens-and-cyberbullying-2022/) The teacher's post was about her student's relationship with AI. The response was about the teacher.*

*The moderation review took 11 hours and 12 minutes. The doxxing posts were live for approximately 8 hours before any were removed. [Content moderation at scale relies on contractors — often in the Philippines, Kenya, and India — who review reported content at rates of 500–1,000 pieces per shift for wages between $1.50 and $4.50 per hour.](https://time.com/6247678/openai-chatgpt-kenya-workers/) [The moderation contractor for Vantage in this world is Clarity Solutions](https://clarity-solutions-queue-volume-alert-2032), a fictional company based on [real outsourced moderation firms like Sama, Majorel (now Teleperformance), and Accenture's content moderation divisions.](https://www.theverge.com/2019/2/25/18229714/cognizant-facebook-content-moderator-interviews-trauma-working-conditions-arizona) The agent who reviewed this case — from Manila, processing their Nth case of the shift — correctly determined the original post did not violate policy. The doxxing that resulted from its amplification was a separate matter, handled separately, hours later.*

*[In 2023, Twitter/X's content moderation team was reduced by approximately 80% following the platform's acquisition.](https://www.nytimes.com/2023/02/26/technology/twitter-layoffs-content-moderation.html) [Meta reduced its content moderation workforce by thousands across 2022–2023.](https://www.theverge.com/2022/11/9/23449217/meta-layoffs-thousands-employees) [The global content moderation market was valued at $8.2 billion in 2023, projected to reach $32 billion by 2031.](https://www.alliedmarketresearch.com/content-moderation-solutions-market) The platforms spend billions moderating content. The algorithm that created the problem in the first place — that identified a teacher's post as high-engagement and showed it to 2.3 million people — is not part of the moderation review. The amplification is a feature. The consequences are a support ticket.*
