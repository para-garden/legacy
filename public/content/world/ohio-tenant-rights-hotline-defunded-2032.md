### Public Notice — Ohio Tenant Rights Hotline

**Effective July 1, 2032**

&nbsp;

The Ohio Tenant Rights Hotline (1-888-555-0162) has been **discontinued** effective July 1, 2032, due to the expiration of federal Emergency Rental Assistance Program (ERAP) funding and [corresponding reductions in state housing services appropriations](https://nlihc.org/era-dashboard).

The Hotline operated from 2021 to 2032 as a partnership between the Ohio Housing Finance Agency (OHFA), Legal Aid of Ohio, and the [Ohio Poverty Law Center](https://www.ohiopovertylaw.org/). During its operation, the Hotline provided:

- Free legal information to Ohio tenants regarding [lease terms, rent increases, habitability standards, and eviction procedures](https://www.ohiolegalhelp.org/)
- Referrals to local legal aid organizations for eligible low-income tenants
- Multilingual assistance (English, Spanish, Somali, Arabic)

**In its final year of operation (FY 2031–32), the Hotline received approximately 47,000 calls.** [Approximately 62% of callers reported that the Hotline was their first point of contact for housing-related legal assistance.](https://nlihc.org/housing-needs-by-state/ohio)

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: My landlord raised my rent. Is this legal?**

Ohio does not have statewide rent control or rent stabilization laws. [No Ohio municipality currently has rent control ordinances in effect.](https://www.ohiolegalhelp.org/) Landlords may raise rent by any amount at the end of a lease term or with proper notice for month-to-month tenancies, provided the increase is not discriminatory or retaliatory. There is no cap on the size of a rent increase.

**Q: My landlord is not making repairs. What can I do?**

Under [Ohio Revised Code §5321.04](https://codes.ohio.gov/ohio-revised-code/section-5321.04), landlords are required to maintain rental properties in a habitable condition. Tenants may place rent in escrow with the local court if the landlord fails to address habitability issues after written notice. Contact your local municipal court clerk's office for escrow procedures.

**Q: I received an eviction notice. Where can I get help?**

Contact Legal Aid of Ohio at 1-888-534-1432 for emergency eviction defense. [In Ohio, a landlord must file an eviction action in court; self-help evictions (changing locks, removing belongings, shutting off utilities) are illegal.](https://www.ohiolegalhelp.org/)

**Q: Can my landlord refuse to renew my lease?**

In most cases, yes. Ohio law does not require landlords to renew leases. A landlord may decline to renew for any non-discriminatory, non-retaliatory reason.

*The Ohio Tenant Rights Hotline was funded through a combination of [Emergency Rental Assistance Program (ERAP)](https://home.treasury.gov/policy-issues/coronavirus/assistance-for-state-local-and-tribal-governments/emergency-rental-assistance-program) funds, [Legal Services Corporation (LSC)](https://www.lsc.gov/) grants, and state general revenue appropriations.
