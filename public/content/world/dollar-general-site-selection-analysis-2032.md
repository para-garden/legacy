The last independent grocery in the Evarts trade area (Piggly Wiggly franchise) closed in 2029. The IGA in Wallins Creek closed in 2027. [Since 2012, Harlan County has lost 4 of its 6 grocery stores.](https://www.ers.usda.gov/data-products/food-access-research-atlas/) The remaining options are a Dollar General in Cumberland and the Walmart in Middlesboro (22-minute drive on US-119, weather permitting).

**Competitive assessment:** No direct competitor within the trade area. Store will serve as the **primary source of food and household goods** for approximately 4,200 residents.

### Recommendation

**APPROVE.** Store #22847 meets all site selection criteria. Low population is offset by **zero competition within the trade area** and high SNAP participation. Projected payback of 14 months is well within corporate threshold (18 months).

The closure of the Piggly Wiggly franchise in 2029 removed the last full-service grocery option in the Evarts trade area. Residents currently drive 11+ miles for grocery shopping or rely on the Cumberland Dollar General. Store #22847 addresses this gap.

*[Dollar General operates approximately 20,000 stores across the United States](https://corporate.dollargeneral.com/investors) — more locations than Walmart, Starbucks, and McDonald's. The company opens roughly [1,000 new stores per year.](https://corporate.dollargeneral.com/investors) Dollar General's stated strategy is to locate stores within 3–5 miles of customers in [rural and low-income communities](https://corporate.dollargeneral.com/investors), areas that traditional grocers have abandoned.*

*[Researchers have documented that Dollar General store openings are correlated with independent grocery store closures in the same trade areas.](https://ilsr.org/report/dollar-store-impacts/) [A 2023 Institute for Local Self-Reliance report](https://ilsr.org/report/dollar-store-impacts/) found that dollar stores "saturate communities and undercut local grocers, contributing to the very food deserts they claim to address." [At least 75 U.S. cities and counties have passed moratoriums or restrictions on dollar store development](https://ilsr.org/dollar-store-harms-map/) — but not Harlan County.*

*[Harlan County, Kentucky, has a poverty rate of 38.4%](https://data.census.gov/table/ACSST5Y2022.S1901), more than double the national average. [Life expectancy in Harlan County is 70.2 years](https://www.countyhealthrankings.org/) — 7.6 years below the national average. [The county's population has declined 42% since 1980.](https://data.census.gov/) Joy Callahan ran a community meal program in Evarts until [2032](https://harlan-county-gazette-callahan-obituary-2032). Store #22847 will open approximately 0.4 miles from the church where the meals were held.*
