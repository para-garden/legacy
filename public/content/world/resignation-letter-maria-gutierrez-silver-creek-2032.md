May 1, 2032

Ms. Harmon,

I am writing to let you know that my last day at Silver Creek will be May 15. I am giving two weeks notice as required.

I want you to know this is not because I don't care about the residents. I know you know that but I wanted to say it anyway. I care about them. Mrs. Patterson on the second floor calls me "mi hija" even though I've told her I'm not her daughter and she says "close enough." Mr. Chen shows me pictures of his grandkids every single shift like I haven't seen them before and I look at them every time like it's the first time because it makes him happy. These are real people and I know their names and I know how they take their coffee and I know which ones can't sleep and need someone to just sit with them for five minutes at 2 AM.

I cannot do this job for $15.25 an hour.

I work night shift. There are six of us for 108 residents. When someone calls off — and someone always calls off — there are five of us. Last Tuesday there were four. Four people for 108 residents. I changed eleven diapers between midnight and 2 AM. My back has not stopped hurting since November. I went to urgent care and they said it was a muscle strain and I should rest and I said when and they didn't have an answer.

I applied at Amazon in Akron. They start at $19. It is not a job I care about. I do not love boxes. But the boxes do not call out for help at 3 AM and I cannot get to them because I am helping someone else and I have to choose who waits. I do not want to choose who waits anymore.

I got my CNA at Ridgemont Community College. My instructor said this was important work. She was right. It is important and it pays $15.25 an hour and Amazon pays $19 to move boxes and that is everything you need to know about how this country decides what matters.

I am sorry. I know this makes things harder for you and I know you are doing your best. The residents deserve better than what we can give them with the staff we have.

Please tell Mrs. Patterson I said goodbye. I know she'll forget but tell her anyway.

Maria Gutierrez, CNA
