## Allied Health & Nursing Assistant Programs

**Your Career in Healthcare Starts Here**

Ridgemont Community College's Allied Health Division prepares students for [in-demand healthcare careers](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/healthcare/nursing-assistants.htm) through hands-on training, expert instruction, and direct pathways to employment. Our Nursing Assistant (STNA) program has placed graduates in healthcare facilities across central and southern Ohio since 2004.

---

### State-Tested Nursing Assistant (STNA) Certificate

**Duration:** 8 weeks (80 contact hours: 59 classroom + 21 clinical)\
**Schedule:** Evenings, Monday/Wednesday/Thursday, 5:30–9:30 PM\
**Location:** Ridgemont Health Sciences Building, Room 108\
**Clinical Site:** Silver Creek Rehabilitation & Nursing Center, Whitmore, OH\
**Start Dates:** January, March, June, August, October (five cohorts per year)

This [state-approved training program](https://odh.ohio.gov/know-our-programs/nurse-aide-training-and-competency-evaluation/nurse-aide) prepares students to provide direct patient care in nursing homes, hospitals, assisted living facilities, and home health settings. Graduates are eligible to sit for the Ohio STNA competency examination administered by [D&S Diversified Technologies](https://www.hdmaster.com/testing/cnatesting/ohio/ohcnahome.htm).

### What You'll Learn

- Vital signs measurement and documentation
- Patient hygiene, bathing, and grooming assistance
- Safe patient transfers and mobility assistance ([proper body mechanics to prevent injury](https://www.osha.gov/nursing-home))
- Nutrition and feeding assistance
- Infection control and standard precautions
- Communication with patients, families, and healthcare teams
- [Patient rights and dignity](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/quality-safety-oversight-general-information/nursing-home-quality/residents-rights) in long-term care settings
- End-of-life care fundamentals
- Medical terminology basics

Clinical hours are completed at Silver Creek Rehabilitation & Nursing Center under direct instructor supervision. Students work alongside current staff to gain real-world experience in a [skilled nursing facility setting](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/certification-compliance/nursing-homes).

### Career Outlook

Nursing assistants are [among the most in-demand healthcare workers in Ohio and nationally](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/healthcare/nursing-assistants.htm). The Bureau of Labor Statistics projects 5% job growth through 2032, with approximately 220,200 openings per year due to growth and replacement needs.

**Median wages (2023 BLS data):**

| Setting | Median Hourly |
|---|---:|
| [Nursing homes / skilled nursing facilities](https://www.bls.gov/oes/current/oes311131.htm) | $17.41 |
| Hospitals | $18.57 |
| Home health services | $15.72 |
| [Ohio median (all settings)](https://www.bls.gov/oes/current/oes_oh.htm) | $16.49 |

**Career advancement:** The STNA certificate is the [first step on the nursing career ladder](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/healthcare/nursing-assistants.htm#tab-6). Many of our graduates advance to Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN) and Registered Nurse (RN) programs. Ridgemont offers both:

- **Practical Nursing Certificate** (LPN) — 12 months, day program
- **Associate Degree in Nursing** (ADN/RN) — 2 years

*Ask your advisor about the STNA-to-LPN bridge pathway.*
