Only **22.3%** was documented as formally collected and recycled. The remaining ~48 million tonnes were dumped, traded, or processed under unregulated conditions.

[A 2016–2018 GPS tracker study by the Basel Action Network](https://www.ban.org/trash-transparency) placed tracking devices inside e-waste delivered to US recyclers, including R2-certified facilities. **40% of tracked items were exported**, many to developing countries, despite recyclers' certifications. Items appeared in Hong Kong, China, Thailand, Pakistan, and Ghana.

[The WHO reports](https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240023901) that **18 million children** work in the informal waste sector globally, many handling e-waste. Documented health effects in e-waste processing workers include elevated blood lead levels, reduced lung function, DNA damage, and increased rates of spontaneous abortion and premature birth. [A 2007 study in Guiyu, China](https://ehp.niehs.nih.gov/doi/10.1289/ehp.9697) found **81.8% of children** in the e-waste processing area had elevated blood lead levels.
