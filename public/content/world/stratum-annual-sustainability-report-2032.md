### A Letter from Our CEO

When I founded Stratum in 2028, I made a promise: we would scale AI infrastructure without sacrificing the planet. Four years later, I'm proud to say we are keeping that promise.

This year, Stratum reached **68% renewable energy** across our facility portfolio. We are on track for our commitment of **carbon neutrality by 2040**. Our Mesa campus — one of the largest AI compute facilities in the Southwest — operates at a Power Use Effectiveness of **1.34**, [well below the industry average of 1.58](https://uptimeinstitute.com/resources/research-and-reports/uptime-institute-global-data-center-survey-results-2023).

None of this is easy. The demand for compute is growing faster than anyone predicted. Our customers — some of the most important AI companies in the world — are asking for more capacity every quarter. We are building as fast as we can. But we are building *right*.

— Ari Mehta, CEO & Co-Founder

&nbsp;

**68% renewable.** We've tripled our contracted solar capacity through long-term power purchase agreements, including a new 200 MW partnership with the [Mesquite Solar Complex](https://www.eia.gov/electricity/data/eia860/) in Maricopa County.

**100% e-waste recycling.** Every decommissioned server is processed through [R2-certified recyclers](https://sustainableelectronics.org/r2-standard/), ensuring responsible material recovery and data destruction.

**$12M invested in water innovation.** We're piloting closed-loop cooling at our new Colo-West facility, which we expect to reduce water consumption by 40% per kWh compared to our existing evaporative systems.

### Water Stewardship

Water is the resource we think about most. Our Mesa facility operates in [one of the fastest-growing metropolitan areas in the country](https://www.census.gov/newsroom/press-releases/2024/subcounty-metro-micro-estimates.html) — and one of the driest. We take that responsibility seriously.

| | |
|---|---:|
| Total water withdrawal (2032) | 1.48 billion gallons |
| Municipal supply share | 94.2% |
| Reclaimed / recycled water | 5.8% |
| [Water Use Effectiveness](https://uptimeinstitute.com/resources/research-and-reports/uptime-institute-global-data-center-survey-results-2023) | 1.79 L/kWh |

We are committed to becoming **water positive by 2038** through investments in watershed restoration, recycled water infrastructure, and next-generation cooling technology. In 2032, we contributed **$2.4 million** to [the Salt River Project's watershed restoration fund](https://www.srpnet.com/) and the [Arizona Water Bank Authority](https://waterbank.az.gov/).

### Looking Ahead

By 2035, we plan to:
- Reach **90% renewable energy** across all facilities
- Deploy closed-loop cooling at all new builds
- Achieve **water positivity** through offsets and reclaimed water
- Eliminate Scope 1 emissions entirely

The world needs more compute. We're going to build it — and we're going to build it responsibly.

*Stratum Technologies consumed [847 GWh of electricity in 2032](https://www.iea.org/energy-system/buildings/data-centres-and-data-transmission-networks) — equivalent to powering [approximately 78,000 average U.S. households](https://www.eia.gov/tools/faqs/faq.php?id=97&t=3) for a year. [U.S. data centers consumed an estimated 176 TWh of electricity in 2023, approximately 4.4% of total U.S. electricity consumption](https://www.iea.org/energy-system/buildings/data-centres-and-data-transmission-networks), a figure projected to double by 2030.*

*The "68% renewable" figure includes [Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs)](https://www.epa.gov/green-power-markets/renewable-energy-certificates-recs) purchased separately from the electricity they represent. The facility draws power from the grid. The grid in Maricopa County is [44% natural gas, 15% coal](https://srpnet.com/). The RECs mean someone, somewhere generated renewable energy. The facility's emissions are reported using the "market-based" accounting method, [which the GHG Protocol permits](https://ghgprotocol.org/scope-2-guidance). The "location-based" number — what actually came out of the power lines — is 98,200 metric tons of CO₂e per quarter, or roughly [392,800 metric tons per year](https://www.epa.gov/egrid).*

*[1.48 billion gallons of water](https://www.washingtonpost.com/climate-environment/2024/09/26/data-centers-water-use-drought/) is approximately 4,540 acre-feet — enough to supply [roughly 9,000 Arizona households for a year](https://www.cap-az.com/). The City of Mesa, where Stratum's primary facility operates, is [entirely dependent on Colorado River water allocations that have been cut by 21% since 2022](https://www.usbr.gov/lc/region/g4000/hourly/rivops.html). The "water positive by 2038" commitment uses the same offset logic as carbon neutrality: fund restoration elsewhere, continue withdrawing locally.*

*The $5.75 million in community investment represents 0.4% of Stratum's 2032 revenue. The 12 remaining on-site employees at Colo-East, down from 34 after [workforce optimization](https://stratum-workforce-reduction-email-2032), were not mentioned in the sustainability report.*
