### February 2032 State Survey Findings

The Ohio Department of Health conducted an unannounced survey on February 14–16, 2032. Silver Creek received the following deficiency citations:

| Tag | Description | Scope/Severity |
|---|---|---|
| [F725](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/quality-safety-oversight-general-information) | Sufficient nursing staff — facility did not maintain sufficient nursing staff to meet resident needs | D (isolated, no actual harm, potential for more than minimal harm) |
| [F684](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/quality-safety-oversight-general-information) | Quality of care — resident not provided necessary treatment | D |
| [F686](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/quality-safety-oversight-general-information) | Treatment/services to prevent/heal pressure ulcers — 2 residents with Stage 2 pressure ulcers identified | E (pattern, no actual harm, potential for more than minimal harm) |

**Plan of correction** was submitted March 1, 2032 and accepted by the Department of Health. Key corrective actions:

1. Recruitment of 4 additional CNA FTEs (in progress — 2 hired as of April 1, 2 positions remain open)
2. Implementation of wound care audit protocol on all shifts
3. Assignment of a dedicated wound care nurse (RN) — currently fulfilled by charge nurse on day shift due to staffing constraints

### Compensation and Recruitment

Current CNA compensation:

| | |
|---|---|
| **Starting wage** | [$15.25/hour](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/healthcare/nursing-assistants.htm) |
| **Average wage (all CNAs)** | $16.10/hour |
| **Annual salary (full-time, no overtime)** | $33,488 |
| **Benefits** | Medical (employee contribution $180/month), dental, 401(k) after 1 year |
| **Shift differential** | Evening: +$0.50/hr · Night: +$1.00/hr |

The labor market in Whitmore/Summit County remains challenging. [The BLS projects 21% growth in home health and personal care aide positions through 2032](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/healthcare/home-health-aides-and-personal-care-aides.htm), reflecting demand far exceeding supply. Amazon's Akron fulfillment center ($19/hour starting, no certification required) and regional hospital systems ($17–$20/hour for CNAs with benefits) continue to draw from the same candidate pool.

We have expanded recruitment to include the Ridgemont Community College CNA certification program and have budgeted for a $500 sign-on bonus for Q2 hires. Retention remains the primary challenge — of 28 CNAs hired in 2031, 22 separated within six months. Exit interview data (where available — response rate is 31%) cites workload, compensation, and scheduling inflexibility as primary drivers.

---

### Workforce Fluidity — Context

I want to frame the staffing numbers in context for Regional. Silver Creek's turnover and coverage gaps are not an operational failure — they reflect [a structural labor market reality](https://www.ahcancal.org/Data-and-Research/Pages/default.aspx) affecting every skilled nursing facility in our region. We are competing for a limited pool of certified aides against employers who can offer higher wages, lower patient loads, and less physically demanding work.

The CNAs who stay at Silver Creek stay because they care about the residents. That is not a sustainable workforce strategy, but it is the truth of what is currently holding this facility together. I want to be transparent about that.

**Recommendation:** I request Regional's approval to increase the starting CNA wage to $16.50/hour (a 8.2% increase, estimated annual cost impact of $48,000) and to approve overtime authorization for night shift coverage during the current vacancy period. Without these adjustments, I cannot guarantee adequate coverage for the next 90 days.
