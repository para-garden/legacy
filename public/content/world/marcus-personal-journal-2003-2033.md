*[Recovered from a plastic bin in a storage unit, unit 14-C, Clarksville Self-Storage, Clarksville, TN. Fourteen composition notebooks, various colors. Most entries dated. A few pages water-damaged. Transcribed as written, including spelling errors in earlier entries. Some entries are short — a few lines. Some have no entry at all for more than a year.]*

---

## September 4, 2003

School started today. I am in 4th grade. My teacher is Mrs. Pelton. She seems okay. She gave us each a worksheet with our name already on it which I thought was cool. There are 28 kids in my class which is too many she said. She said that herself.

Mom was still sleeping when I left. She works nights at the hospital, not like a nurse but in the kitchen part. I made my own breakfast which is fine. I know how to do cereal.

---

## November 12, 2003

Darius got a new bike. It's red. We rode around the parking lot of the church for like two hours after school. His cousin was there too. It was good.

We were supposed to go to the science museum on the field trip but it got cancelled because not enough parents could drive. Mrs. Pelton said maybe in spring. She has said that before.

---

## March 3, 2004

I got a B on the reading test. Mom said that's good. She put it on the refrigerator which is what she does. She was tired when she got home, she works the early-morning shift now which is better than nights but she's still tired.

I needed help with the math worksheet. I waited but she fell asleep on the couch so I just did it myself and got some wrong. I'll ask Darius tomorrow.

---

## October 19, 2004

5th grade now. New teacher, Mr. Hendricks. He's young and tries hard. He brought in books from his own house because we don't have enough and you have to share. I don't mind sharing but it's hard when you want to look something up and someone else has it.

Darius says his sister goes to the school on the other side of Route 9 and they each have their own books and also computers. I don't know if that's true. Maybe it is. Darius doesn't usually lie.

---

## February 7, 2006

I am in 7th grade. I haven't written in a long time, I lost the other notebook I think.

Nothing is very different. Mom got a different job at the laundry place on Clement which is days and closer to home. She likes it better. We got a new TV, the old one the sound was going.

I made the basketball team. Not the A team, the second team, but still.

---

## May 22, 2008

Just finished 9th grade. I don't know what to write. Everything feels kind of flat right now. End of the year always feels that way.

I took the practice test, the state one. My scores were okay in English and not great in math. Ms. Burrows said not to worry about it. She says that a lot.

Darius didn't make it to 10th grade, he has to repeat. He's not that stupid, I don't think it's that. He just stopped coming a lot in the spring.

---

## October 30, 2009

SAT prep class. School offers it free, which is supposed to be good. There are 30 kids and one teacher and she goes kind of fast. I'm keeping up but I feel like some stuff I should have already known. Like we're catching up to where we were supposed to be.

Darius works at his uncle's shop now. He seems okay with it.

Homecoming was last weekend. I went with a girl named Simone. It was pretty good. Her dress was purple.

---

## March 15, 2010

Met with the guidance counselor today. Mrs. Frye. She's [the only one for the whole school](https://www.schoolcounselor.org/About-School-Counseling/School-Counselor-Roles-Ratios) which is 800 kids. I waited for 40 minutes and then we had like 20 minutes.

She looked at my grades and scores and asked what I was thinking. I said maybe college. She said that was good, she said there are "a lot of pathways." She asked about trade programs, did I know about the welding certification, the HVAC program at the community college, they have good job placement, she had the brochures right there.

I don't know. She wasn't mean about it. She seemed like she was trying to help. She said the community college was "a smart choice for someone in my situation." I wrote that down.

I think she's probably right. I don't know what my situation is exactly but she's been doing this a long time.

---

## June 8, 2010

Graduated. Mom came and Aunt Cheryl. We took pictures in the parking lot.

I got a job at the Home Depot on Millfield, stocking. [$9.50 an hour](https://www.bls.gov/oes/2005/may/oes412031.htm). I start Tuesday.

I'm going to look into the HVAC thing in the fall. See how much it costs.

---

## August 14, 2012

Enrolled in the HVAC program. It took two years to save up for it. The program is eighteen months. I'm doing it nights, working days at the warehouse now, the Home Depot let me go last year when they restructured.

It's a lot. I'm tired most of the time. But the program feels useful. You learn actual things. The instructors know what they're talking about.

I think this is the right move.

---

## April 4, 2014

Got the certification. Got a job with a company called Meridian Climate Services. Commercial accounts. The pay is better — [$17.50](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/installation-maintenance-and-repair/heating-air-conditioning-and-refrigeration-mechanics-and-installers.htm) to start, with the promise of moving up.

I'm going to get my own place.

---

## September 27, 2015

I moved to Clarksville. I know it sounds strange, I just picked it. I wanted somewhere that wasn't here, that's all. I looked at apartment listings for three cities and Clarksville came up the cheapest. I drove up there with my stuff in a rented van and found a job within two weeks — another HVAC company, Taylor & Sons, better than Meridian honestly.

My apartment is on the third floor. Small. But it's mine.

I feel like something is different. Like I'm outside something I've been inside my whole life. I don't know how to explain it. I walk around and the city doesn't know anything about me. That feels good.

---

## January 3, 2016

I've been thinking about the neighborhood. The apartment I found is cheap — cheap for a reason, I know, but I was looking for cheap. Some of the streets around here are fine. Some aren't.

I asked my coworker Dale what he thought. He said it was "transitional." He said it depends on which school your kids are in. I don't have kids. I said yeah, sure.

---

## October 12, 2018

Her name is Yolanda. She's from here, grew up on the north side. We've been together almost a year. She's smart in a way that isn't loud about it, the kind of person who has already thought about the thing before you finish saying it.

I took her to this place on Fifth Street and she ordered the wrong thing and spent twenty minutes trying to be polite about not liking it and we both started laughing. That was the night I thought, okay, this is it.

---

## March 30, 2020

Zoe was born on March 28th. Two days ago. I keep writing that like I'll understand it if I write it enough.

7 lbs 4 oz. She was angry when she came out, just screaming, and the doctor laughed and said she's got opinions. I thought, good. She should have opinions.

Yolanda is amazing. I don't know how else to say it. I watched her do this thing that I cannot imagine doing and then she held Zoe and she looked calm. She looked like she knew exactly what she was supposed to do.

I held Zoe for a long time. She smelled like something I don't have a word for.

---

## August 19, 2021

Back to something like normal, or the new normal, everyone says that now. Yolanda went back to work in the spring. We found a daycare — [it costs almost exactly what she makes](https://www.childcareaware.org/our-issues/research/the-us-and-the-high-price-of-child-care-2019/) four days a week. We don't talk about that.

Zoe is walking now. She's not good at it but she's committed. She will walk straight into a wall and get up and go again. She doesn't seem to register failure as a concept.

Work is fine. Taylor & Sons made me a lead tech. Small raise.

---

## May 5, 2024

Zoe is four. She talks constantly. Mostly about a show she likes with a rabbit and also about why certain foods are "bad ones." She has very strong opinions about the bad ones.

Mom came up and stayed for a week. She and Zoe were inseparable. Zoe kept calling her "Grandma Big" because she's tall, and Mom loved it.

I was quiet a lot while Mom was here. Watching her with Zoe. Thinking about things I don't know how to write.

---

## September 8, 2026

Zoe started first grade. She walked in and didn't look back, just went right to the table with the crayons.

The school seems fine. It's older, the building — Yolanda noticed the hallway ceilings are water-stained in places. The class has 26 kids. The teacher, Ms. Abara, seems good, she had a whole welcoming letter and everything.

I asked about the parent-teacher nights. Last year was her first year there, I think. You can tell she cares.

---

## February 21, 2028

Zoe's reading above grade level, the report card says. They flagged it like it was a concern, or no — not a concern, just something they noticed. They don't have a program for it is what Ms. Abara said at the conference. There used to be a [gifted program but it was cut](https://www.nytimes.com/2021/10/08/nyregion/gifted-talented-program-nyc.html).

She does read a lot. We get books from the library every Saturday, that's our Saturday thing. She reads them in about four days and then she's ready to go back.

I don't know. I feel like something should happen because of the reading thing, but I can't figure out what.

---

## November 14, 2029

Yolanda's been saying we should look at the school situation for middle school. Zoe will be eligible in two years. There's a magnet program across town — you have to apply, test, the whole thing. She found out about it from someone at work.

I looked into it. The deadline for the test registration was October 15th. It's now November. We missed it.

There's always next year.

---

## March 2, 2031

We applied to the magnet program. Zoe took the test in January. She was nervous, she said, because she didn't know what kind of problems they'd ask.

I read something online while I was waiting to hear back. Someone on a forum was talking about how the kids who score well on the magnet test are usually the ones who've done test prep, and the test prep centers are $200-$400 for a course, and if you didn't know to do that you're just going in cold. Zoe went in cold.

She didn't get in.

Yolanda says it's fine. We find the best option from where we are. That's what we do.

---

## August 31, 2031

Zoe starts middle school on Wednesday. Jefferson Middle. It's the school in our district.

I drove past it last week. It looks like my middle school looked, which I don't know what to do with. Same kind of portable classrooms around the back. Same kind of parking lot.

I'm not going to say that to Yolanda.

---

## September 12, 2031

Zoe came home and said the science teacher assigned a textbook but the textbook is from 2016 and she could already tell some of the things in it were wrong. She said it matter-of-factly, like she was just reporting information. I said, how do you know? She said she'd read about it at the library.

I said: I'll look into it. See if there's a newer one we can get.

She said: it's fine, Dad.

She sounded older than eleven.

---

## January 20, 2032

The guidance counselor situation. Yolanda found out there's one counselor for all three grades — 480 kids. She emailed to schedule a check-in for Zoe and they said the earliest available was late March.

I keep starting to say something about this and then not finishing the sentence.

---

## April 17, 2032

Long day. Did three commercial jobs back to back, one out in the county that took forever to get to. Came home and Zoe was at the kitchen table doing homework and Yolanda was on a work call in the bedroom. I meant to sit down with Zoe and see where she was. I made something to eat and sat down and she was almost done and I thought, she's fine, she doesn't need me hovering.

She went to bed. I watched something. Yolanda came out at nine and we talked about her work thing.

I keep meaning to sit down with Zoe on the homework. She seems to be doing fine. I just don't want to assume.

---

## June 4, 2032

Zoe finished 6th grade. She got a 3.4. Not bad.

Her homeroom teacher, Mr. Veloz, stopped me at pickup on the last day and said she was one of the sharp ones, that I should make sure she stays challenged. He said it sort of quietly, like he was telling me something I needed to know.

I said what do you mean. He said, just keep her reading. Keep her asking questions. Make sure she has things outside of school.

I thanked him. I drove home. I kept thinking about how he said *outside of school* specifically. Like that was where it was going to have to happen.

---

## September 5, 2032

Zoe's in 7th grade now. She didn't want to talk about the first day much. I asked and she said it was fine. I asked again at dinner and she said "Dad, it's fine."

New year. Trying to keep track better, be more present. I've been working a lot — the company took on two big contracts and I'm lead on both, which is good money but I'm tired by the time I get home. Yolanda's carrying a lot. We don't fight about it, we just both notice it.

---

## February 14, 2033

Valentine's Day. Yolanda and I went out, first real dinner in a while. She ordered something with too many flavors in it and it was wrong in a good way. We talked for three hours. Not about Zoe's school, not about money. Just talked.

She said she was happy. I said I was happy. I think we both meant it.

---

## May 19, 2033

Something good happened and I want to write it down.

The school does this thing called the Young Scholars Showcase — student projects, displayed in the gym. Zoe did a project on water access and sanitation in the district, like actually here, this city. She made a tri-fold board but she also made a little data visualization on the computer, printed it out. Charts. She labeled everything.

She stood by her project for two hours and talked to everyone who came by. Parents, teachers, other students. I watched her explain it to a man who was clearly a little skeptical — I could tell by how he was standing — and she just walked him through the data. Patient. Precise. He was nodding by the end.

She didn't get first place. A kid named Trevor got first place, his project had a working model with circuits. But Mr. Veloz — he was one of the judges — he came up to me afterward and said Zoe had the best methodology of anyone in the room. He said methodology specifically. I didn't know what to say so I said thank you.

She got a certificate. Honorable Mention. She put it in her backpack.

In the car she said: I should have done the circuits.

I said: You'll know for next year.

She said: I know I'll know. That's not helpful right now.

I said okay. I said it was a really good project. She said yeah. We drove home. She had the certificate on her knee the whole way, like she forgot she was holding it.

I'm proud of her. She's something.

I'm already thinking about next year — what I can find for her over the summer, programs, things to keep her going. There's supposed to be a STEM camp at the community college, I saw a flyer. I need to look into whether there's financial aid.

I'll find something. She's going to be fine. I'm going to make sure of it.
