## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Stratum, Inc. is an AI infrastructure company founded in 2029 through the consolidation of three earlier ventures. The company operates high-density compute facilities optimized for large-scale AI model training and inference. We recommend leading the $2.4 billion Series B with an $800 million commitment, representing our largest single investment in Fund VII.

We believe Stratum represents a generational infrastructure opportunity. The core thesis is simple: **demand for AI compute is growing faster than supply, the gap is widening, and the companies that control compute capacity will control the value chain.**

This is not speculative. This is infrastructure.

## MARKET THESIS

### The Compute Bottleneck

AI capability improvement is a direct function of compute. [Scaling laws](https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361) established in the early 2020s have held through every generation of large language models and continue to hold as models expand into multimodal reasoning, agentic workflows, and domain-specific applications. The relationship is predictable: more compute produces more capable models.

Global AI compute demand is growing at approximately [4–5× annually](https://www.iea.org/reports/electricity-2024/executive-summary). Datacenter capacity, constrained by power, cooling, land, and permitting timelines, is growing at roughly 30–40% annually. **The gap between demand and supply is widening every quarter.** Every major AI company is compute-constrained. The waitlist for NVIDIA H100/H200 allocation exceeds 18 months for most customers.

Stratum's insight: rather than building AI models (high R&D risk, winner-take-most dynamics), build the infrastructure that *every* AI company needs. [Compute is the new oil.](https://www.economist.com/leaders/2017/05/06/the-worlds-most-valuable-resource-is-no-longer-oil-but-data) The refineries are the business.

### Why This Is Not a Bubble

We are aware of the comparisons to previous technology cycles. We have examined them carefully.

The dotcom bubble was driven by revenue-free companies valued on eyeballs. AI infrastructure companies have [contracted revenue](https://www.reuters.com/technology/artificial-intelligence/openai-closes-66-billion-funding-haul-2024-10-02/) from the most sophisticated technology buyers in the world — hyperscalers, governments, pharmaceutical companies, financial institutions. Stratum's order book is $4.2 billion for the next 24 months, with 90% from investment-grade counterparties.

The crypto mining parallel is equally inapplicable. Crypto compute was single-purpose and stranded the moment mining economics shifted. AI compute serves a broad and diversifying customer base across training, inference, fine-tuning, and retrieval-augmented workloads. **Even if model architectures change, the compute requirement per unit of capability has only increased with every architectural advance to date.**

To be direct: the risk is not that this market doesn't exist. The risk is that we don't move fast enough to capture our share of it.

## RISK FACTORS

We have identified and evaluated the following risks:

**Technological disruption.** If a fundamentally more compute-efficient architecture emerges, demand for raw compute could plateau. We assess this risk as low for the investment horizon. [Every previous efficiency gain](https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361) (distillation, quantization, mixture-of-experts) has been absorbed by expanded use cases, resulting in net *increased* compute demand. This dynamic — known as the [Jevons paradox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jevons_paradox) — has held consistently since the beginning of the transformer era.

**Power and permitting.** Datacenter construction is constrained by electrical grid capacity, water availability for cooling, and local permitting timelines. Stratum has secured power purchase agreements for 80% of planned capacity through 2031. Remaining facilities are in advanced permitting stages with strong local government relationships.

**Capital intensity.** This is a capital-intensive business. The bull case requires continued access to large-scale financing at reasonable terms. Stratum's CFO has demonstrated the ability to access diverse capital pools. The sovereign wealth interest alone provides a backstop that most infrastructure companies cannot match.

**Regulatory.** AI regulation is evolving. We do not view this as a material risk to infrastructure specifically — regulation targets model developers and deployers, not compute providers. Stratum is a utility, not an application.
