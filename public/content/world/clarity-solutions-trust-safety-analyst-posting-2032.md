### What You'll Do

As a Trust & Safety Analyst, you'll play a critical role in maintaining the integrity of our platform partners' communities. Your day-to-day responsibilities include:

- **Review user-generated content** for compliance with platform partner community guidelines, terms of service, and applicable legal requirements
- **Apply content policies consistently** across categories including but not limited to: hate speech, violent threats, harassment, misinformation, regulated goods, and [graphic or sensitive media](https://www.theverge.com/2019/6/19/18681845/facebook-moderator-interviews-video-trauma-ptsd-cognizant-tampa)
- **Render content decisions within established timeframes** to maintain platform safety at scale
- **Escalate complex or ambiguous content** to senior reviewers and subject matter experts using internal tooling
- **Document decisions** and provide rationale consistent with internal quality standards
- **Participate in regular policy update sessions** as platform partner guidelines evolve
- **Maintain quality and accuracy benchmarks** in a fast-paced, high-volume environment

You will undergo comprehensive training on partner-specific policies during your first two weeks. Ongoing calibration sessions ensure alignment across the team.

**Required:**

**Preferred:**

- Familiarity with social media platforms and online community dynamics
- Bilingual candidates encouraged to apply (Spanish, Portuguese, Mandarin, Arabic, or Tagalog preferred — bilingual differential of $0.75/hour)
- Associates degree or relevant certification

### Work Environment

This role is performed on-site at our Austin Content Operations Center. The facility includes:

- Dedicated quiet rooms available to all team members
- On-site wellness coordinator during business hours
- Team-based seating with supportive supervision

This position involves extended periods of seated computer work and requires sustained attention to screen-based content. **Content reviewed in this role may include material depicting violence, abuse, self-harm, hate speech, and other disturbing subject matter.** [No current federal workplace safety standard specifically addresses the psychological hazards associated with content review work.](https://worklaw.jotwell.com/why-employees-need-the-osh-act-and-oshas-protection-from-psychological-harm-due-to-unsafe-workplaces/)

Clarity Solutions is committed to supporting the well-being of our team members. All Trust & Safety Analysts have access to our EAP and on-site wellness resources. [Research indicates that individuals in content review roles may experience elevated rates of psychological distress](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38153846/); we encourage all applicants to consider the nature of this work carefully before applying.
