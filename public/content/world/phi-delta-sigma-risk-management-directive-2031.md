TO: All Chapter Presidents
FROM: Office of Risk Management
DATE: November 3, 2031
RE: Updated Social Event Protocols — Immediate Implementation Required

Brothers,

Following the events of October and in consultation with our [national insurance carrier](https://nicfraternity.org), the following protocols are effective immediately and apply to all chartered chapters and colonies.

**Social Event Registration**

All social events must be registered with the chapter's Risk Management Officer no fewer than 72 hours in advance. Unregistered events are uninsured events. Chapters that host unregistered events will be referred to the Standards Board.

**Sober Monitor Requirements**

A minimum of three (3) designated sober monitors are required for any event with attendance over 50. Sober monitors must be identifiable (vests are acceptable), must not consume alcohol or controlled substances during the event, and must be briefed on the chapter's emergency response protocol before the event begins.

**Guest List Documentation**

Written or digital guest lists must be maintained for all events and retained for a minimum of 90 days. In the event of an incident, the chapter president is responsible for making this documentation available to the national office within 24 hours of request.

**Incident Reporting**

Any incident requiring emergency services response must be reported to the national office within 4 hours. "Incident" includes but is not limited to: medical emergency, law enforcement contact, property damage requiring insurance claim, or formal complaint from a guest or neighbor.

Failure to report within this window affects the chapter's ability to access national legal support resources.

**Chapter Health Review**

Chapters currently under Standards Board review are reminded that compliance with these protocols is a condition of continued charter. Quarterly chapter health reports will include a social event compliance section beginning Spring 2032.

We understand this places additional administrative burden on chapter officers. These measures exist to protect the chapter, its members, and the fraternity's standing.

Questions should be directed to riskmanagement@phideltasigma.org.

In brotherhood,

Marcus Ellison
Director, Risk Management
Phi Delta Sigma National Office
