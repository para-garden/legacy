**For context:** U.S. self-storage industry revenue ($44B) exceeds the [domestic box office](https://www.boxofficemojo.com/) (~$11B), the [recorded music industry](https://www.riaa.com/) (~$17B), and the [U.S. video game market](https://www.theesa.com/) (~$57B in software, but ~$40B excluding mobile).

There is [more self-storage space in the United States](https://www.sparefoot.com/self-storage/news/self-storage-industry-statistics/) than the total retail square footage of Walmart, Target, and Costco combined.

### Customer Insights (Annual Survey, n=12,400)

**Primary reason for renting a unit:**

| Reason | % |
|---|---:|
| Moving / in transition | 24% |
| [Decluttering / not enough space at home](https://www.selfstorage.org/) | 31% |
| Storing seasonal items | 14% |
| Business inventory / documents | 12% |
| Life event (divorce, death, downsizing) | 11% |
| Renovation / temporary displacement | 8% |

**Average duration of rental:** 14 months

**Key finding:** 47% of customers surveyed said they had **not visited their unit in the past 3 months**. 18% had not visited in **over 12 months**. The most common items stored: furniture (68%), boxes of unsorted belongings (54%), seasonal decorations (42%), clothing (39%), sporting equipment (31%).

When asked "could you list the specific items in your unit from memory?", **62% said no**.

### Growth Drivers

1. **E-commerce and returns:** The surge in online purchasing continues to generate household inventory that exceeds living space. [The average American home contains approximately 300,000 items.](https://www.becomingminimalist.com/clutter-stats/) Self-storage absorbs the overflow.

2. **Housing affordability:** As [rent increases outpace wage growth](https://www.jchs.harvard.edu/), households are downsizing to smaller units while retaining possessions from larger spaces. This dynamic is particularly strong in markets where SafeGuard operates (Sun Belt, Midwest).

3. **Life events:** Divorce, death, and aging-related downsizing continue to drive demand. The aging Baby Boomer cohort represents a sustained, multi-decade growth tailwind.

4. **Behavioral inertia:** Customer retention is high because the cost of visiting, sorting, and removing stored items often exceeds the perceived cost of another month's rent. **Monthly churn is 3.8%**, meaning the average customer stays 26 months before terminating — significantly longer than the 14 months they initially intend.

### Expansion Pipeline

SafeGuard has 18 new facilities under development, with projected delivery dates in 2033–2034. Target markets: Phoenix, AZ; Austin, TX; Nashville, TN; Columbus, OH; Charlotte, NC.

The Phoenix metro area alone has added [4.2 million square feet of new self-storage](https://www.yardi.com/blog/self-storage/) in the past three years. Current per-capita self-storage supply in Phoenix is approximately 9.8 square feet per person.
