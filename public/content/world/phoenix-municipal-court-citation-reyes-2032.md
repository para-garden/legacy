### Options

**Option 1: Pay the fine**

Pay $293.65 by the due date. [Two (2) points will be assessed to your driving record](https://azdot.gov/motor-vehicles/driver-services/driver-improvement/point-system) by the Arizona Department of Transportation. Points remain on your record for 12 months.

**Option 2: Defensive driving school**

You may be eligible to attend a [court-approved defensive driving school](https://www.phoenix.gov/court/defensive-driving) in lieu of points and a portion of the fine. Eligibility requires:
- No defensive driving school attendance in the past 24 months
- No commercial vehicle violations
- Violation was not committed in a school zone

If eligible, the fine is reduced to the base amount of $155.00 plus a $48.00 school fee. **Total: $203.00.** Points are not assessed. [Defensive driving school is approximately 4 hours and must be completed within 60 days.](https://www.azcourts.gov/selfservicecenter/defensive-driving)

**Option 3: Contest the citation**

You may request a hearing to contest this citation. Hearings are scheduled within 60–90 days. You may represent yourself or retain an attorney. [Traffic attorneys in Phoenix typically charge $250–$500 for a contested photo enforcement citation.](https://www.avvo.com/legal-answers/how-much-does-a-traffic-ticket-lawyer-cost-4064723.html)

### Point System — Impact on Rideshare Driving

*Note: This section is not part of the official citation. It is included as general information.*

[Rideshare platform partners (Uber, Lyft) may deactivate drivers who accumulate traffic violations.](https://www.uber.com/legal/en/document/?name=general-community-guidelines&country=united-states&lang=en) Uber's community guidelines state that drivers must maintain a clean driving record. [Two or more moving violations within a 12-month period may result in account review or deactivation.](https://www.uber.com/legal/en/document/?name=general-community-guidelines&country=united-states&lang=en) Deactivation decisions are made by the platform and are generally final.

Arizona assesses the following [point values for common moving violations](https://azdot.gov/motor-vehicles/driver-services/driver-improvement/point-system):

| Violation | Points |
|---|---:|
| Red light violation | 2 |
| Speeding (10+ over) | 3 |
| Failure to yield | 3 |
| Following too closely | 4 |
| Reckless driving | 8 |

Accumulating 8 or more points within a 12-month period triggers a mandatory [Traffic Survival School order](https://azdot.gov/motor-vehicles/driver-services/driver-improvement/point-system).
