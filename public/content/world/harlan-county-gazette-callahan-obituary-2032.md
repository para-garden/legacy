**Evarts Woman Behind Community Meals Dies at 31**

*By Sarah Combs, Staff Reporter*

Joy Callahan, 31, of Evarts, died Saturday at Harlan ARH Hospital. The Harlan County Coroner's Office confirmed the death but declined to release additional details, citing family privacy.

Callahan was known locally for organizing an informal meal program that served dozens of households across Evarts and surrounding communities. What began in 2028 as cooking for a recovering neighbor grew into a volunteer effort that prepared and delivered meals three to four nights a week, coordinated primarily through [social media posts](https://www.today.com/health/news/youtube-star-mikayla-raines-29-dies-suicide-bullying-rcna214973).

"She just showed up with food," said Bobby Sloane, 64, of Evarts. "Nobody asked her to. She just did it."

At its peak, the program involved roughly fifteen volunteers and served as many as forty households in a given week. Callahan funded the operation through personal income, occasional donations, and a GoFundMe campaign that raised approximately $11,000 over two years. She was not affiliated with any church or nonprofit organization.

The effort gained visibility beyond Harlan County after several of Callahan's posts were widely shared on social media, attracting a following of several thousand. A Lexington television station profiled the program in early 2031.

That visibility also attracted scrutiny. In recent months, an [online community](https://www.thecut.com/article/youtube-star-mikayla-raines-dies-by-suicide-at-29.html) dedicated to discussing Callahan and her program had grown to several thousand members. Former volunteers were among the participants. The community's posts — individually within the platform's [terms of service](https://www.usmagazine.com/celebrity-news/news/petition-targets-reddit-snark-page-after-saveafoxs-mikayla-raines-suicide/) — included criticism of the program's finances, Callahan's motives, and her personal life.

Callahan's mother, Diane Callahan of Middlesboro, declined to be interviewed for this article. In a brief statement provided through a family friend, she said: "Joy fed people. That's what she did. I would like people to remember that."

A petition calling for the removal of the online community was circulating as of Monday and had collected approximately 6,200 signatures. The platform had not taken action as of press time.

Several volunteers said they intend to continue the meal program. Tammy Hoskins, who has helped with deliveries since 2030, said meals went out as scheduled on Tuesday.

"Somebody's got to," Hoskins said.

Callahan is survived by her mother, Diane Callahan of Middlesboro; her sister, whose name the family asked to be withheld; and her dog, Biscuit.

Services will be held Thursday at 2:00 PM at Evarts Church of God. In lieu of flowers, the family requests donations to the [Harlan County Food Pantry](https://feedingamerica.org).
