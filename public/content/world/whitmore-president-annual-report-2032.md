## Message from the President

Dear Whitmore Community,

It is my privilege to share with you the highlights of what has been, by every measure, a landmark year for Whitmore University.

This year we enrolled **14,237 undergraduate students** — the largest incoming class in our institution's 168-year history, and a 4.2% increase over the prior year. Our six-year graduation rate has risen to [**68.4%**](https://nces.ed.gov/programs/coe/indicator/ctr/undergrad-retention-graduation), the highest on record, reflecting years of investment in student support services, advising technology, and our nationally recognized First-Year Experience program.

Our incoming class represents **all 50 states and 47 countries**. The share of first-generation college students has reached **34%**, a figure we are deeply proud of. These students bring perspectives and resilience that enrich every classroom and community space on our campus.

### Academic Excellence

Whitmore faculty published **1,847 peer-reviewed articles** and secured **$112 million in external research funding** — a 9% increase over the previous fiscal year, led by growth in our biomedical engineering and data science programs. The College of Engineering was ranked among the [top 50 public engineering programs](https://www.usnews.com/best-colleges/rankings/engineering) by *U.S. News & World Report* for the third consecutive year.

We added **26 new tenure-track faculty lines** across four colleges, with particular strength in computer science, public health, and interdisciplinary studies. We remain committed to attracting and retaining the caliber of scholars our students deserve.

### Campus Transformation

The **Meridian Center for Student Innovation** opened in September — a [$94 million, 180,000-square-foot facility](https://www.constructiondive.com/news/higher-education-construction-spending-billion-2023/696432/) housing collaborative workspaces, a maker lab, a 400-seat auditorium, and the new home of our entrepreneurship program. Meridian was designed with student input at every stage, and I'm told the third-floor commons has already become the most popular study space on campus. I've confirmed this personally — there are never empty seats.

Phase II of the **North Campus Residential Village** is on track for fall 2033 delivery, adding 1,200 beds to our housing inventory and bringing our first-year housing guarantee to 100%.

### Financial Stewardship

The Whitmore endowment closed the fiscal year at **$1.34 billion**, reflecting a [5.8% return](https://www.nacubo.org/Research/2023/Public-NCSE-Tables) and continued generosity from our alumni and friends. Annual giving participation among recent graduates (classes 2027–2031) reached **22%**, well above the [national average of 6%](https://www.case.org/resources/voluntary-support-education). The Together Tomorrow campaign surpassed its $400 million goal eighteen months ahead of schedule.

I am especially grateful for the 3,241 donors who gave to our **Whitmore Access Fund**, which provides emergency grants to students facing unexpected financial hardship — car repairs, medical bills, family emergencies. These small interventions, typically [$500–$1,500](https://www.luminafoundation.org/resource/beyond-financial-aid/), have measurable impact on persistence and graduation.

### Student Experience

Our Division I athletics programs earned a conference-record **NCAA Academic Progress Rate** of 989. Student organizations grew to 412 registered groups. The Center for Community Engagement logged **184,000 hours** of student service.

The Office of Equity and Civil Rights Compliance processed all formal complaints within the timelines established by university policy and federal regulation. We continue to invest in prevention programming, bystander intervention training, and the support infrastructure our students and employees deserve.

### Looking Ahead

I have spent thirty-one years in higher education, and I believe deeply that what we do here matters — that a Whitmore degree changes the trajectory of a life. Our metrics confirm this: **92% of graduates** report being employed or in graduate school within six months of commencement. The [median starting salary](https://www.payscale.com/research/US/School=Whitmore_University/Salary) for a Whitmore bachelor's degree holder has risen to **$52,400**, and our graduates carry an average student loan debt of [**$31,100**](https://educationdata.org/average-student-loan-debt) — below the national average of $33,500.

These numbers represent real people, real futures, and the real impact of a Whitmore education. I am honored to lead this community, and I am energized by the work ahead.

With gratitude,

**Katherine A. Pruitt, PhD**
*President, Whitmore University*
