**Emily Schwartz** @emilyschwartzDC · Jul 18, 2032

A housing policy contact forwarded me an investor letter from Heron Capital Partners, a PE firm that owns ~14,200 apartment units in OH, KY, IN, TN, and GA.

I need you to read some of these quotes. Thread:

🧵 1/

---

**Emily Schwartz** @emilyschwartzDC · Jul 18

"Revenue per unit increased 19.1% year-over-year across the Fund II portfolio, driven by [algorithmic pricing optimization](https://www.propublica.org/article/yieldstar-rent-increase-realpage-rent), strategic unit upgrades, and disciplined concession management."

"Algorithmic pricing optimization" = a computer raised everyone's rent.

2/

---

**Emily Schwartz** @emilyschwartzDC · Jul 18

"Turnover at the property level, while elevated relative to pre-acquisition benchmarks, has been a net positive for revenue growth. Vacated units are renovated and re-leased at market rates, typically at a 30–40% premium over legacy rents."

They are telling their investors that tenants leaving is GOOD FOR RETURNS.

3/

---

**Emily Schwartz** @emilyschwartzDC · Jul 18

"Turnover is a value-creation event."

That's a direct quote. A person losing their home is a "value-creation event."

4/

---

**Emily Schwartz** @emilyschwartzDC · Jul 18

The fund's managing partner — [Harvard MBA, marathon runner, youth soccer coach](https://www.nmhc.org/), food bank board member — describes the thesis as "market-rate solutions to the housing affordability challenge."

You're buying affordable housing and making it unaffordable. That's not a solution. That's the problem wearing a Patagonia vest.

5/

---

**Emily Schwartz** @emilyschwartzDC · Jul 18

One of their properties, Caldwell Place in Whitmore, OH, has a [2.3-star average on Google](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-25-106701). Tenants report 22% rent increases, maintenance tickets closed without repairs, and a management office that tells them the "revenue management system" sets the price and they can't adjust it.

6/

---

**Emily Schwartz** @emilyschwartzDC · Jul 18

The fund has returned 22.1% net IRR to investors on Fund I. Fund II is projected at 18.6%.

That return is coming from somewhere. It's coming from people who now can't afford their apartment. The mechanism is precise: buy it, raise the rent, renovate when they leave, re-lease at 30-40% more.

7/

---

**Emily Schwartz** @emilyschwartzDC · Jul 18

I want to be clear: none of this is illegal. Every sentence in this letter describes legal activity. The [DOJ has sued algorithmic pricing platforms](https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/justice-department-sues-realpage-algorithmic-pricing-scheme-harms-millions-american-renters) but the landlords using them are not currently defendants. [Ohio has no rent control laws.](https://www.ohiolegalhelp.org/) There is no cap on rent increases. There is no required justification.

8/

---

**Emily Schwartz** @emilyschwartzDC · Jul 18

The letter closes with: "We remain committed to creating value for our investors and the communities we serve."

The investors got 22.1% IRR. What did the community get?

9/9
