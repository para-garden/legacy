### Item 1: Summer Water Use Report

Director Nolan presented the Summer 2032 water use summary. [Lake Mead remains at 1,038 feet](https://www.usbr.gov/lc/region/g4000/hourly/rivops.html) — 22 feet above the first critical threshold triggering additional allocation cuts to Arizona under the [2019 Drought Contingency Plan](https://www.usbr.gov/dcp/). [Arizona's Colorado River allocation has been cut by approximately 21% since 2022.](https://www.usbr.gov/lc/region/g4000/hourly/rivops.html)

| | |
|---|---:|
| Total municipal water use (June–August 2032) | 18.4 billion gallons |
| [Residential use](https://www.mesaaz.gov/residents/water) | 62.4% |

| [Industrial / data center](https://www.mesaaz.gov/residents/water) | 11.8% |
| Parks, golf courses, municipal | 3.7% |

**Largest single industrial user:** Stratum Technologies, Colo-East Campus — [1.48 billion gallons (annualized)](https://stratum-annual-sustainability-report-2032), representing approximately **2.1% of Mesa's annual allocation** from the [Salt River Project](https://srpnet.com/).

### Item 2: Public Comment Period

**Speaker 1: Patricia Morales, Mesa resident (Val Vista/Main)**

Ms. Morales asked why residential customers are subject to mandatory Stage 3 water restrictions while Stratum Technologies operates under a voluntary reduction target. She noted that her block received a warning notice for excess residential use in July. "I have four kids and we're taking five-minute showers and that data center is using nine thousand households' worth of water. How is that voluntary?"

Director Nolan responded that the Stratum water agreement, signed in 2028, predates the current restriction framework and includes a fixed allocation through 2039. Renegotiation would require City Council action.

**Speaker 2: David Moreno, Mesa resident (Dobson Ranch)**

Mr. Moreno asked how much Stratum pays per gallon compared to residential customers.

| | Rate per 1,000 gallons |
|---|---:|
| [Residential (Tier 1, 0–5 CCF)](https://www.mesaaz.gov/residents/water) | $4.23 |
| [Residential (Tier 3, 15+ CCF)](https://www.mesaaz.gov/residents/water) | $8.47 |
| [Commercial / Industrial (flat rate)](https://www.mesaaz.gov/residents/water) | $3.18 |

Director Nolan confirmed that industrial water rates are **lower** than residential Tier 1 rates. The rate structure was approved by City Council in 2019 as part of the economic development incentive package for the Stratum campus. [Stratum's estimated annual water cost: $4.7 million.](https://stratum-colo-east-environmental-compliance-q3-2032)

**Speaker 3: Margaret Reyes, Mesa resident (Alma School/Baseline)**

Ms. Reyes asked whether the city had conducted an environmental impact assessment before approving the Stratum water agreement. City Attorney Suresh responded that the agreement was classified as an economic development incentive and was not subject to NEPA review. Ms. Reyes: "So nobody checked."

**Speaker 4: Alan Kuo, representing Mesa Chamber of Commerce**

Mr. Kuo spoke in support of Stratum's continued operations, noting the company's $5.75 million in community investments and its role as Mesa's largest commercial taxpayer.

---

### Item 3: Board Discussion

Member Mahmoud asked whether the city could impose mandatory reduction targets on industrial users under Stage 4 restrictions. Director Nolan said Stage 4 would require City Council declaration of a water emergency and could trigger force majeure clauses in existing water agreements.

Member Begay asked how many residential disconnect notices had been issued in 2032 for nonpayment.

| | |
|---|---:|
| Residential disconnect notices (Jan–Aug 2032) | 4,218 |
| Residential disconnections (actual) | 1,847 |
| Average outstanding balance at disconnect | $284 |
| [Reconnection fee](https://www.mesaaz.gov/residents/water) | $50 |

Member Begay asked if Stratum had ever received a disconnect notice. Director Nolan: "No."

---

### Item 4: Adjournment

Motion to adjourn by Member Garcia-Wells, seconded by Member Christiansen. The board adjourned at 8:42 PM.

*Next regular session: September 19, 2032.*

*[Microsoft's water consumption jumped 34% in 2022, tied directly to AI training workloads.](https://fortune.com/2023/09/09/ai-chatgpt-usage-fuels-spike-in-microsoft-water-consumption/) [U.S. data centers consumed an estimated 17 billion gallons of water directly in 2023](https://www.eesi.org/articles/view/data-centers-and-water-consumption), projected to double by 2028. [Arizona's Colorado River allocation — the primary water source for Mesa, Phoenix, and Tucson — has been reduced by 21% since 2022 due to declining reservoir levels.](https://www.usbr.gov/lc/region/g4000/hourly/rivops.html)*

*1,847 Mesa households had their water disconnected in the first eight months of 2032. The average outstanding balance was $284. The reconnection fee is $50. Stratum Technologies used 1.48 billion gallons and paid an estimated $4.7 million at a rate lower than residential customers pay. It has never received a disconnect notice.*
