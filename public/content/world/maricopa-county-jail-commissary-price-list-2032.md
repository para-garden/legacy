At $0.63/hour, it requires 1.7 hours. [Most inmates rely on family members to deposit funds.](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/blog/2018/08/09/commissary/)*

### How to Deposit Funds

Family and friends may add funds to an inmate's commissary account by:
1. **Online:** [www.accesscorrections.com](https://www.accesscorrections.com/) (3.5% fee)
2. **Lobby kiosk:** 4th Avenue Jail, Lower Buckeye Jail ($3.00 fee)
3. **Phone:** 1-866-345-1884 ($5.95 fee)
4. **Money order:** Mail to MCSO Inmate Services (allow 7–10 business days)

*All deposit methods carry fees. [There is no free way to send money to an incarcerated person.](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/blog/2018/08/09/commissary/)*

*[U.S. jail and prison commissary sales total approximately $1.6 billion annually.](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/blog/2018/08/09/commissary/) [Keefe Group (a subsidiary of H.I.G. Capital) and three other companies control approximately 80% of the market.](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/blog/2018/08/09/commissary/) Commissary contracts typically include a revenue-sharing agreement in which the jail or sheriff's office receives [a commission of 15–30% of all sales.](https://worthrises.org/theprisonindustry) The Maricopa County Sheriff's Office received approximately $4.2 million in commissary commissions in fiscal year 2031.*

*[Families of incarcerated people spend an estimated $2.9 billion per year on commissary and phone calls.](https://ellabakercenter.org/who-pays-the-real-costs-of-incarceration-by-the-numbers/) [83% of these costs are borne by women — mothers, wives, girlfriends, and sisters.](https://ellabakercenter.org/who-pays-the-real-costs-of-incarceration-by-the-numbers/) [The median annual income of families with an incarcerated member is $48,000, roughly half the national median.](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/reports/income.html)*

*[The Maricopa County Jail system holds approximately 7,000 people on any given day.](https://www.mcso.org/) [Approximately 72% are pretrial — not convicted, awaiting trial.](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/reports/pie2024.html) They buy ramen at $1.05 because the jail food is [approximately 2,000 calories per day](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/reports/food.html) and frequently insufficient. The ramen costs 3.75x retail. They have no other store. The store knows this.*
