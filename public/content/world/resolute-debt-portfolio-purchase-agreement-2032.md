### Seller Representations

The Seller represents that:

1. All accounts have been through the Seller's internal collections process for a minimum of **120 days** before being placed in this portfolio.
2. The Seller has provided the Buyer with **name, address, date of birth, Social Security number, account balance, date of service, and service description** for each account.
3. The Seller does not warrant the accuracy of account holder contact information, which may be outdated.
4. [Accounts are sold "as-is."](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/research-reports/medical-debt-burden-in-the-united-states/) The Seller makes no representations regarding collectibility.
5. The Seller retains the right to **recall accounts** where litigation or regulatory action is pending.

---

### Buyer Obligations

The Buyer agrees to:

1. Comply with the [Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA)](https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/statutes/fair-debt-collection-practices-act) and all applicable state collection laws.
2. Send a [written validation notice](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/what-is-a-debt-validation-letter-en-1920/) within 5 days of first contact with each account holder.
3. Not misrepresent the nature of the debt or the Buyer's relationship to the original creditor.
4. Maintain records of all collection activity for a minimum of 7 years.

*[Approximately 100 million Americans carry medical debt.](https://www.kff.org/health-costs/issue-brief/the-burden-of-medical-debt-in-the-united-states/) [Total U.S. medical debt is estimated at $220 billion.](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/research-reports/medical-debt-burden-in-the-united-states/) [Medical bills are the leading cause of personal bankruptcy in the United States.](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6366487/) [The medical debt collection industry purchases portfolios at an average of 3–7 cents per dollar of face value](https://www.ftc.gov/sites/default/files/documents/reports/structure-and-practices-debt-buying-industry/debtbuyingreport.pdf) and pursues the full balance from the debtor, plus fees and interest where state law allows.*

*[The CFPB reported in 2022 that 58% of all third-party debt collection entries on credit reports are for medical bills.](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/research-reports/medical-debt-burden-in-the-united-states/) [In 2023, the three major credit bureaus removed medical debts under $500 from credit reports](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/newsroom/), affecting approximately 15 million consumers. Debts above $500 — which constitute the majority of medical debt by dollar volume — continue to appear.*

*Account 4782-008 is a pediatric asthma ED visit. The patient is a child. The balance is $2,840. The child's parent — uninsured, Maricopa County — brought their child to the emergency department because [federal law (EMTALA) requires emergency rooms to treat all patients regardless of ability to pay](https://www.cms.gov/regulations-and-guidance/legislation/emtala). The hospital treated the child and sent the bill. The parent did not pay within 120 days. The hospital sold the account to Resolute for approximately $114. Resolute will pursue $2,840.*
