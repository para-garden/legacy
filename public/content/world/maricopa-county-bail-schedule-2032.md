### Standard Bail Schedule — Effective January 1, 2032

*[Pursuant to Arizona Rules of Criminal Procedure, Rule 7.2](https://www.azcourts.gov/rules/), the following bail amounts are established for initial appearance. Judicial officers may modify bail based on individual circumstances, flight risk, and [public safety considerations](https://www.azcourts.gov/).*

---

#### Class 1 Misdemeanors

| Offense | Standard Bail |
|---|---:|
| Assault (no weapon, no serious injury) | $1,000 |
| Disorderly conduct | $500 |
| DUI (first offense) | $2,500 |
| DUI (second offense) | $5,000 |
| Criminal damage (< $1,000) | $1,000 |
| Theft (< $1,000) | $500 |
| Drug possession — personal use (marijuana) | $1,000 |
| Trespass (first degree) | $1,000 |
| Shoplifting (< $1,000, first offense) | $500 |
| Domestic violence — misdemeanor | $2,500 |
| Resisting arrest | $2,500 |

#### Class 6 Felonies (lowest felony class)

| Offense | Standard Bail |
|---|---:|
| Theft ($1,000–$2,000) | $2,500 |
| Criminal damage ($2,000–$10,000) | $5,000 |
| Forgery | $5,000 |
| Drug possession — personal use (non-marijuana) | $2,500 |
| Aggravated DUI (3rd within 7 years) | $10,000 |
| Possession of drug paraphernalia | $1,000 |

#### Class 4–5 Felonies

| Offense | Standard Bail |
|---|---:|
| Theft ($3,000–$25,000) | $10,000 |
| Burglary (third degree — nonresidential) | $10,000 |
| Aggravated assault (no weapon) | $15,000 |
| Forgery (multiple counts) | $15,000 |
| Drug possession for sale (< threshold) | $15,000 |

#### Class 2–3 Felonies

| Offense | Standard Bail |
|---|---:|
| Burglary (first degree — residential) | $25,000 |
| Aggravated assault (weapon) | $50,000 |
| Armed robbery | $50,000 |
| Drug trafficking | $75,000–$500,000 |
| Sexual assault | $75,000–$150,000 |
| Attempted murder | $250,000–$1,000,000 |

### Posting Bail

| Method | Cost to Defendant |
|---|---|
| **Cash bail** | Full amount, [refunded upon case disposition](https://www.azcourts.gov/) (minus administrative fee) |
| **[Surety bond (bail bondsman)](https://www.insurance.az.gov/)** | **10% non-refundable premium** — e.g., $500 bail = $50 premium, $10,000 bail = $1,000 premium |
| **Property bond** | Real property valued at 1.5x bail amount; lien filed |
| **Own recognizance (OR release)** | $0 — judicial discretion, based on flight risk assessment |

*[If the defendant cannot post bail or secure a bond, the defendant is held in the Maricopa County Jail](https://www.mcso.org/) until the next scheduled court appearance, which may be 3–7 business days. For felony charges, pretrial detention may extend weeks or months pending trial.*

---

### What Happens If You Can't Pay

A defendant arrested on a **$500 misdemeanor theft charge** who cannot post bail or secure a $50 bond will:

1. Be held in the [Maricopa County 4th Avenue Jail](https://www.mcso.org/) for a minimum of **24–72 hours** until initial appearance.
2. Potentially remain incarcerated for **3–7 days** until the next available court date.
3. Upon release (if OR is granted at hearing), may have already:
   - Missed scheduled work shifts
   - Incurred vehicle towing and storage fees ($180–$350)
   - Lost childcare arrangements
   - Lost housing (many leases allow termination after arrest)

*[On any given day, approximately 470,000 people are held in U.S. jails pretrial — not convicted of any crime, but unable to afford bail.](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/reports/pie2024.html) [Approximately 74% of people in local jails have not been convicted.](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/reports/pie2024.html) They are legally innocent. They are incarcerated because they cannot pay.*

*[A 2022 study found that defendants held pretrial for even 2–3 days were 25% more likely to plead guilty](https://www.arnoldfoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/Pretrial-Criminal-Justice-Research-Brief.pdf) than those released, regardless of the strength of the evidence — because pleading guilty is the fastest way to go home. [Pretrial detention increases the likelihood of a guilty plea by 13% for misdemeanors and 9% for felonies.](https://www.arnoldfoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/Pretrial-Criminal-Justice-Research-Brief.pdf) The system does not distinguish between "guilty" and "cannot afford to wait."*

*[The commercial bail bond industry generates approximately $2.4 billion in annual revenue.](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/reports/bail_lobby.html) [The United States and the Philippines are the only two countries in the world with a commercial bail bond industry.](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/reports/bail_lobby.html) The 10% bond premium is non-refundable — even if the charges are dropped, the defendant does not get the money back.*

*[A Maricopa County public defender handles 400–550 cases simultaneously.](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/legal_aid_indigent_defense/indigent_defense_systems_improvement/standards-and-policies/) [The ABA recommends a maximum of 150 felony cases or 400 misdemeanor cases per year.](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/legal_aid_indigent_defense/indigent_defense_systems_improvement/standards-and-policies/) [A 2023 RAND study estimated that public defenders spend an average of 6 minutes per case on misdemeanor charges in high-volume jurisdictions.](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports.html) Six minutes includes file review, client interview, and court appearance. The right to counsel is guaranteed. The right to counsel that has time to read the file is not.*
