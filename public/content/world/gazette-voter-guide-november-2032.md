**Your Complete Guide to the November 2032 Ballot**
*Everything Wilkes County voters need to know before Election Day*

### Wilkes County, Ohio — General Election, November 8, 2032

The following guide covers all races and ballot measures appearing on the Wilkes County ballot. Early voting begins October 24 at the Wilkes County Board of Elections, 200 N. Main St., Clarksburg. Polls are open 6:30 AM to 7:30 PM on Election Day. Bring a valid photo ID.

---

## Federal Races

### U.S. Senate — Ohio

**Margaret Chen (D) — Incumbent.** First elected 2026. Serves on Finance and Health committees. Key positions: supports prescription drug pricing reform, infrastructure investment, and expanded broadband access. Endorsed by Ohio AFL-CIO.

**David Harmon (R) — Challenger.** Former state attorney general (2022–2026). Key positions: fiscal responsibility, reduced regulatory burden, border security, parental rights in education. Endorsed by Ohio Farm Bureau.

### U.S. House — Ohio 12th District

**James Alcott (R) — Incumbent.** Serving fourth term. Serves on Agriculture and Small Business committees. Voted for the Farm Stability Act. Supports maintaining current tax policy and reducing federal spending.

**Patricia Okafor (D) — Challenger.** Wilkes County commissioner since 2028. Focuses on healthcare access, rural broadband, and public school funding. Endorsed by Ohio Education Association.

## Wilkes County Issues

### Issue 1 — Wilkes County Emergency Services Levy Renewal

**A renewal of the existing 2.5-mill levy** for emergency medical services and fire protection. First passed in 2022. Generates approximately $3.2 million annually. Funds 42 full-time paramedics and EMTs across 6 stations.

**For:** Maintains current EMS response times (average 8.4 minutes in rural areas). No new taxes — this is a renewal of an existing levy. Failure would require service reductions estimated at 30%.

**Against:** Property taxes remain high relative to county median income. Some voters prefer consolidation with adjacent county EMS services for efficiency.

*The Gazette recommends: Yes on Issue 1.*

### Issue 2 — Rezoning: Former Wilkes Manufacturing Site

**Proposed rezoning** of the 47-acre former Wilkes Manufacturing site on County Road 12 from Industrial to Mixed-Use Commercial. The current owner, Resolute Development Group, has submitted preliminary plans for a retail and warehouse complex.

**For:** Brings development and jobs to a site that has been vacant since 2024. Estimated 180 construction jobs, 90 permanent positions. Projected increase in county tax revenue of $420,000 annually.

**Against:** Traffic impact on County Road 12 not fully assessed. Adjacent residential properties may see decreased value. The county comprehensive plan designated this area for light industrial use, and critics argue the rezoning bypasses the planning process. Environmental remediation of the manufacturing site is incomplete.

*The Gazette recommends: No position. Voters should attend the November 1 public hearing at Clarksburg Town Hall for more information.*

### Issue 3 — School District Operating Levy

**A new 4.8-mill operating levy** for Wilkes County School District. Would generate approximately $5.1 million annually for 5 years. The district reports a projected $2.3 million annual shortfall beginning in fiscal year 2034.

**For:** The district has not passed a new operating levy since 2019. Per-pupil spending is $9,400, approximately [15% below the state average](https://nces.ed.gov/programs/coe/indicator/cmd/public-school-expenditures). Class sizes have increased. Two elementary counselor positions were eliminated in 2031.

**Against:** Residents already pay among the highest effective property tax rates in the region. Fixed-income homeowners, particularly retirees, would be disproportionately affected. The district has not demonstrated sufficient cost reduction efforts before requesting additional revenue.

*The Gazette recommends: Yes on Issue 3.*
