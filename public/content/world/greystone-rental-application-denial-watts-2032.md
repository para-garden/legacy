### Your Rights Under Federal Law

This decision was based in whole or in part on information obtained from a consumer reporting agency. Under the [Fair Credit Reporting Act (15 U.S.C. § 1681m)](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/15/1681m), you have the right to:

1. **Obtain a free copy of your credit report** from the agency that provided the report within 60 days of this notice.
2. **Dispute the accuracy** of any information in the report directly with the reporting agency.
3. **Add a consumer statement** to your credit file explaining the circumstances of the reported item.

The consumer reporting agency that provided the report is:

> **TransUnion**
> PO Box 1000, Chester, PA 19016
> (800) 916-8800
> [www.transunion.com](https://www.transunion.com)

TransUnion did not make the decision to deny your application and is unable to provide the specific reasons for this decision. For questions regarding this decision, contact Greystone Properties directly.

---

### Appealing This Decision

If you believe the information on your credit report is inaccurate, or if circumstances have changed, you may submit additional documentation to our office for reconsideration. Reconsideration requests must be submitted within 14 days of the date of this letter and should include:

- A written explanation of the collections account
- Documentation of any payment plan or settlement with the original creditor
- Proof of additional income or assets not reflected in your application

Please note that reconsideration is not guaranteed and is subject to management review.

*Jordan Watts had an [emergency appendectomy at Ascend Regional Medical Center in October 2031](https://ascend-hospital-bill-appendectomy-watts-2031). He was uninsured. [The bill was $41,112.75 after a 25% self-pay discount.](https://ascend-hospital-bill-appendectomy-watts-2031) He did not pay within 120 days. [Ascend sold his account to Resolute Recovery Services as part of a portfolio of 4,847 medical debt accounts](https://resolute-debt-portfolio-purchase-agreement-2032) — face value $38.2 million, purchased for $1.53 million, four cents on the dollar. [Resolute reported the debt to the credit bureaus.](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/research-reports/medical-debt-burden-in-the-united-states/) Jordan's credit score dropped from an estimated 680 to 574.*

*Six months later, Jordan applied for an apartment. The apartment is managed by [Greystone Properties, a Heron Capital portfolio company](https://heron-capital-investor-letter-q3-2032). The application was denied because of the medical collections account. [The apartment he applied for costs $1,750/month](https://greystone-rent-increase-notice-odom-2032) — the same building where [Rachel Odom received a 22% rent increase](https://greystone-rent-increase-notice-odom-2032).*

*[Approximately 100 million Americans carry medical debt.](https://www.kff.org/health-costs/issue-brief/the-burden-of-medical-debt-in-the-united-states/) [58% of all debt in collections on credit reports is medical.](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/research-reports/medical-debt-burden-in-the-united-states/) [Medical debt on a credit report reduces the probability of being approved for credit by 14 percentage points.](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/research-reports/medical-debt-burden-in-the-united-states/) [In 2023, the three major credit bureaus removed medical debts under $500 from reports.](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/newsroom/) Jordan's debt is $41,112.75. [The CFPB finalized a rule in 2025 to remove all medical debt from credit reports. A federal court vacated the rule.](https://gravislaw.com/bankruptcy-law/medical-debt-relief-credit-score-changes/) The debt remains.*

*[Tenant screening companies report credit data to landlords but do not distinguish between medical debt and other debt.](https://www.transunion.com/solution/tenant-screening-services) The screening report shows "collections — $41,112.75." It does not say: appendectomy. It does not say: emergency. It does not say: uninsured. Greystone's criteria require zero active collections accounts. The algorithm does not have a field for "he went to the hospital because his appendix was about to rupture and he didn't have insurance."*
