**Denise M.** · ★☆☆☆☆ · August 2032

My rent went up 22% this year. Twenty. Two. Percent. When I called to ask why, I was told it was set by the "revenue management system" and the office couldn't adjust it. I asked to speak to a manager. The manager said the same thing. I asked who controls the revenue management system. She said corporate. I asked for corporate's number. She gave me an 800 number and I was on hold for 45 minutes and then a recording said to email. I emailed. I got an auto-reply. That was three weeks ago.

I've lived here for four years. I pay on time every month. The hallway lights have been out on the second floor since June. But sure, 22% increase, because the algorithm said so.

**Marcus W.** · ★★☆☆☆ · July 2032

The apartment itself is fine. Updated appliances, decent layout. But the management changed last year — it used to be locally owned, now it's Greystone — and since then everything is different. Maintenance requests go into an app and you get a ticket number and then nothing happens for two weeks. I put in a request for a leaking faucet in May and they closed the ticket without fixing it. I put in a new one. Same thing. Third time I walked into the office and the woman at the desk said "we're transitioning to a new work order system" which I think means nobody is looking at the tickets.

Also: the rent increases. Everyone in the building got hit. The people who've been here longest got hit the hardest because their rents were below what the system says the "market rate" is. So loyalty gets you a bigger increase. That's the deal.
