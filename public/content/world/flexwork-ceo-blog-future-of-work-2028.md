## The Future of Work Is Freedom

*By Jason Park, CEO & Co-Founder*

---

I want to tell you about Maria.

Maria is 34. She lives in Houston. She's a single mom with two kids. For eight years, she worked at a call center — locked into a schedule she didn't choose, doing work that didn't use a fraction of her talent, for a wage that someone else decided she was worth.

Six months ago, Maria joined FlexWork. Today, she works when she wants, where she wants. She picks up her kids from school every day at 3:15. She makes more than she did at the call center. She told me — and I'm quoting her — "I feel like a person again."

This is the future of work. Not a corner office. Not a title. **Freedom.**

---

### The old model is broken

Here's what the traditional employment model actually is: you sell 40 hours of your life every week to a single buyer, at a price they set, on a schedule they dictate, doing tasks they define. You have one customer for your labor. If they decide you're not needed, you have zero customers. There is a word for a business with one customer: fragile.

We built FlexWork because we believe every person deserves to be a business of one. [The gig economy](https://www.bls.gov/spotlight/2018/self-employed-workers/home.htm) isn't a trend. It's a correction. For a hundred years, the corporation was the default unit of work. That was an artifact of industrial-age communication and coordination costs. Those costs are now zero. The smartphone in your pocket is a full-stack business infrastructure — storefront, dispatch, payment processing, customer management, reputation system.

The corporate job was never about the worker. It was about the employer's need to control labor. We just called it "security."

---

### What FlexWork does differently

We're not a gig platform. We're a **work operating system.**

Most platforms match supply and demand and take a cut. That's a marketplace. A marketplace extracts value from both sides.

FlexWork is different. We invest in the worker:

- **FlexLearn**: Free skill development courses so our workers can move up, not just across. Maria started with delivery; she's now doing bookkeeping for three local businesses through the platform.
- **FlexShield**: Access to portable benefits — health insurance, retirement savings, accident coverage — that travel with the worker, not the employer. [Because benefits should belong to people, not companies.](https://aspe.hhs.gov/reports/health-insurance-coverage-gig-economy-workers)
- **FlexRate**: Our pricing algorithm ensures that workers earn a living wage. We set floor rates in every market based on local cost-of-living data. No race to the bottom.
- **FlexVoice**: Worker advisory councils in every city. Our workers aren't just on the platform — they shape it.

We believe that if you build for the worker, the business follows.

---

### The numbers

In two years:
- **340,000 active workers** across 42 cities
- **Average hourly earnings: $24.50** (before expenses)
- **78% of workers** report higher satisfaction than their previous job
- **$2.1 billion** in total worker earnings since launch
- **Series C: $380 million** led by Sequoia, with participation from a]6z, Founders Fund, and SoftBank Vision Fund

We're growing because the market is ready. [The number of Americans doing gig work has grown 30% since 2020.](https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/future-of-work) They're not doing it because they can't find "real" jobs. They're doing it because they've seen what "real" jobs actually offer — and they want something better.

---

### The criticism

I know what the critics say. "Gig companies exploit workers." "There are no protections." "It's precarious."

Let me be direct: the precarity isn't created by the platform. The precarity is the human condition. Every job is precarious. The difference is that a traditional employer hides the precarity behind a benefits package and a two-week notice period. We're honest about it — and then we build tools to address it.

FlexShield gives our workers access to health insurance. Traditional employment gives you health insurance tied to one employer — lose the job, lose the coverage. [The employer-based health insurance system is an accident of World War II–era wage controls.](https://www.nytimes.com/2017/09/05/upshot/the-real-reason-the-us-has-employer-sponsored-health-insurance.html) It was never designed for workers. It was designed for employers who needed to attract labor without raising wages.

We're not the ones who broke the social contract. We're building a new one.

---

### What's next

By 2030, I believe half of all working Americans will earn income through platforms like ours. Not because we won. Because the old way stopped working.

The 9-to-5 is dead. The corner office is dead. The gold watch is dead. What's alive is a 34-year-old woman in Houston who picks up her kids at 3:15 and runs her own life.

That's what freedom looks like. We're just building the infrastructure.

— Jason
