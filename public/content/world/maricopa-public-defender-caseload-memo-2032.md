### ABA Standards Comparison

[The American Bar Association's Ten Principles of a Public Defense Delivery System](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/legal_aid_indigent_defense/indigent_defense_systems_improvement/standards-and-policies/) recommends maximum annual caseloads per attorney:

| Case Type | [ABA / NAC Recommended Maximum](https://www.nlada.org/defender-standards) | Our Current Load | % Over Maximum |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Felony | 150 | 373 | **149% over** |
| Misdemeanor | 400 | 585 | **46% over** |
| Juvenile | 200 | 280 | **40% over** |

The office has exceeded [ABA recommended caseload limits](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/legal_aid_indigent_defense/indigent_defense_systems_improvement/standards-and-policies/) in every category for seven consecutive years.

---

### Time Per Client

Based on available attorney hours (47 attorneys × [2,080 annual working hours](https://www.bls.gov/charts/american-time-use/emp-by-ftpt-sex-edu.htm) × 75% billable allocation = 73,320 hours) divided across 22,400 cases:

| | |
|---|---:|
| Average hours per case (all types) | 3.3 hours |
| Average hours per felony case | 5.1 hours |
| Average hours per misdemeanor case | 1.8 hours |
| Average hours per misdemeanor case (investigation, research, admin removed) | **0.7 hours** |

Effective attorney-client time per misdemeanor case — excluding administrative tasks, court appearances without substantive interaction, and travel — is approximately **42 minutes**. This encompasses intake, case review, client meetings, plea negotiation, and (in the 3% of cases that proceed to trial) trial preparation and representation.

For the approximately 68% of misdemeanor clients who resolve their cases at or before the initial pretrial conference, direct attorney-client time averages **7 minutes**.

---

### Pretrial Detention and Caseload Interaction

[Approximately 72% of the Maricopa County Jail population is pretrial](https://maricopa-county-bail-schedule-2032) — detained because they cannot post bail, not because they have been convicted.

Pretrial clients who remain in custody generate additional demands on attorney time:

| | |
|---|---:|
| Average days in custody before case resolution (misdemeanor) | 18 |
| Average days in custody before case resolution (felony) | 74 |
| [Pretrial detention increases guilty plea likelihood](https://www.arnoldfoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/LJAF_Report_hidden-costs_FNL.pdf) | By 25% |

Clients in custody are more likely to accept plea offers to expedite release — regardless of the merits of the defense. This dynamic compounds the caseload problem: if attorneys had time to investigate and prepare, some percentage of pretrial clients might contest charges rather than plead. The system incentivizes rapid resolution. The caseload ensures it.

---

### Recommendations

1. **Immediate:** Increase starting salary to $72,000 minimum to reduce attrition and close the recruitment gap with the County Attorney's Office. Estimated annual cost: $450,000.
2. **Short-term:** Request authorization for 12 additional attorney positions to bring the felony caseload within 200% of ABA standards. Estimated annual cost: $1.2 million.
3. **Long-term:** Implement a caseload cap consistent with ABA guidelines and [the 2023 *Strickland*-derived caseload standards adopted by several states](https://sixthamendment.org/the-right-to-counsel/right-to-counsel-national-campaign/).

These recommendations are substantially similar to those made in Q2 2031, Q4 2030, and Q2 2030. No additional funding was allocated in response to those requests.

*[The Sixth Amendment to the United States Constitution guarantees the right to counsel in criminal proceedings.](https://www.law.cornell.edu/constitution/sixth_amendment) [In Gideon v. Wainwright (1963), the Supreme Court held that states must provide attorneys to defendants who cannot afford them.](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/372/335/) [In Strickland v. Washington (1984), the Court established a standard for "effective assistance of counsel"](https://supreme.justia.com/cases/federal/us/466/668/) — but courts have rarely found caseload alone sufficient to prove ineffective assistance.*

*[The ABA recommended maximum annual caseload for a public defender is 150 felonies or 400 misdemeanors.](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/legal_aid_indigent_defense/indigent_defense_systems_improvement/standards-and-policies/) [A RAND Corporation study found that in high-volume misdemeanor courts, public defenders averaged approximately 6 minutes of direct contact per case.](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RR1348.html) [A 2023 report from the Sixth Amendment Center found that most public defender offices in the United States operate at caseloads that make effective representation impossible.](https://sixthamendment.org/the-right-to-counsel/right-to-counsel-national-campaign/)*

*[The starting salary for a public defender in Maricopa County is approximately $55,000–$65,000 (as of 2023).](https://www.glassdoor.com/Salaries/phoenix-public-defender-salary-SRCH_IL.0,7_IM678_KO8,23.htm) [The Maricopa County Attorney's Office (prosecution) starts at approximately $72,000.](https://www.glassdoor.com/Salaries/phoenix-county-attorney-salary-SRCH_IL.0,7_IM678_KO8,23.htm) [National average public defender salary is $63,000; national average prosecutor salary is $73,000.](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/legal/lawyers.htm) The pay gap between prosecution and defense is structural: prosecutors are funded by the same county budget as public defenders, but prosecution offices are consistently funded at higher levels.*

*The memo's recommendations — higher salaries, more positions, caseload caps — are described as "substantially similar to those made in Q2 2031, Q4 2030, and Q2 2030." [In 2009, the American Bar Association Standing Committee on Legal Aid and Indigent Defendants published "Eight Guidelines of Public Defense Related to Excessive Workloads," noting that the problem had been documented for decades with no systemic resolution.](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/legal_aid_indigent_defense/indigent_defense_systems_improvement/standards-and-policies/) The guidelines were published 23 years before this memo. The caseloads described in the memo are worse than the ones the guidelines were written to address.*
