### DEPARTMENT OF HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES

**Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services**

**42 CFR Part 483**

**[CMS-3442-F]**

**RIN 0938-AV24**

**Medicare and Medicaid Programs; Repeal of Minimum Staffing Standards for Long-Term Care Facilities**

---

**AGENCY:** Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS), Department of Health and Human Services (HHS).

**ACTION:** Final rule.

**SUMMARY:** This final rule repeals the minimum staffing standards for Medicare- and Medicaid-certified long-term care (LTC) facilities published on May 10, 2024 (89 FR 40875). The repealed rule had established minimum staffing levels of [3.48 total nursing hours per resident per day (HPRD)](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-minimum-staffing-standards-long-term-care-facilities-and-medicaid-0), including a minimum of [0.55 HPRD for registered nurses (RNs)](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-minimum-staffing-standards-long-term-care-facilities-and-medicaid-0) and 2.45 HPRD for nurse aides, and a requirement that a registered nurse be on-site 24 hours per day, 7 days per week.

Upon repeal, the prior statutory standard applies: [a registered nurse must be on-site for a minimum of 8 consecutive hours per day, 7 days per week](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/12/03/2025-21792/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-repeal-of-minimum-staffing-standards-for-long-term-care-facilities). There is no federal minimum for total nursing hours, nurse aide hours, or LPN hours.

**DATES:** Effective January 2, 2026.

---

### SUPPLEMENTARY INFORMATION

#### I. Background

On April 22, 2024, CMS published a final rule establishing, for the first time in the history of the Medicare and Medicaid programs, minimum staffing standards for long-term care facilities. [The rule was informed by approximately 46,000 public comments](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-minimum-staffing-standards-long-term-care-facilities-and-medicaid-0) and supported by [research demonstrating a consistent relationship between higher staffing levels and improved resident outcomes](https://www.healthaffairs.org/doi/10.1377/hlthaff.2020.00957), including reduced pressure ulcers, hospitalizations, urinary tract infections, and mortality.

#### II. Basis for Repeal

After review, CMS has determined that the minimum staffing standards impose costs on long-term care facilities that may reduce access to care, particularly in rural areas. [The American Health Care Association estimated compliance costs of $6.8 billion annually.](https://www.ahcancal.org/) CMS acknowledges the evidence linking staffing levels to quality but concludes that federal minimum standards are not the most effective mechanism for improvement.

Compliance data gathered in May 2024 indicated that at the time of publication:

| Standard | Facilities in Compliance |
|---|---:|
| 0.55 HPRD RN minimum | [50%](https://aspe.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/documents/8e343af705b07063ad7effcc84ba370a/nurse-staffing-estimates-us-nhs.pdf) |
| 3.48 HPRD total nursing | [59%](https://aspe.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/documents/8e343af705b07063ad7effcc84ba370a/nurse-staffing-estimates-us-nhs.pdf) |
| 2.45 HPRD nurse aide minimum | [30%](https://aspe.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/documents/8e343af705b07063ad7effcc84ba370a/nurse-staffing-estimates-us-nhs.pdf) |
| 24/7 RN on-site | [78%](https://aspe.hhs.gov/sites/default/files/documents/8e343af705b07063ad7effcc84ba370a/nurse-staffing-estimates-us-nhs.pdf) |

CMS notes that the nurse aide standard was met by fewer than one in three facilities at the time of publication.

#### III. Effective Date

The repeal is effective January 2, 2026. Facilities that had begun implementation of the staffing standards are not required to maintain those levels.

*[The minimum staffing rule, finalized in April 2024, was the first federal staffing floor for nursing homes in the history of Medicare and Medicaid.](https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-minimum-staffing-standards-long-term-care-facilities-and-medicaid-0) [It was repealed in December 2025.](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/12/03/2025-21792/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-repeal-of-minimum-staffing-standards-for-long-term-care-facilities) It was in effect for 8 months.*

*[The research supporting the rule was extensive: higher RN staffing is consistently associated with fewer pressure ulcers, fewer hospitalizations, fewer urinary tract infections, and fewer deaths.](https://www.healthaffairs.org/doi/10.1377/hlthaff.2020.00957) The repeal did not dispute the research. It cited cost.*

*[The American Health Care Association, the nursing home industry's primary lobbying group, spent $8.5 million on lobbying in 2024.](https://www.opensecrets.org/federal-lobbying/clients/summary?cycle=2024&id=D000022150) [82% of U.S. nursing homes are for-profit.](https://data.cms.gov/provider-data/topics/nursing-homes/) [The five largest for-profit nursing home chains reported combined operating revenue exceeding $12 billion in 2023.](https://skillednursingnews.com/)*

*[Silver Creek Senior Living reported 2.94 total nursing HPRD](https://cms-nursing-home-inspection-silver-creek-2032) — below the repealed minimum of 3.48. Under the rule that existed for 8 months, Silver Creek would have been required to increase staffing by 18.4%. Under the current standard, Silver Creek is in compliance. There is no minimum for total nursing hours. There is no minimum for nurse aide hours. [Maria Gutierrez was one of the nurse aides.](https://resignation-letter-maria-gutierrez-silver-creek-2032)*
