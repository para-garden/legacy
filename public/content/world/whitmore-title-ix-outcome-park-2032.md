**Summary of Process**

Your complaint alleged that a Whitmore University student engaged in non-consensual sexual contact at an off-campus social event on October 14, 2031. The Office of Equity and Civil Rights Compliance conducted a formal investigation pursuant to the university's grievance procedures, which included:

- Intake meeting with the complainant (October 22, 2031)
- Written notice of allegations provided to respondent (October 28, 2031)
- Investigator interviews with complainant (conducted October 22, November 6, and November 19, 2031)
- Investigator interview with respondent
- Interviews with two identified witnesses
- Review of submitted documentary evidence
- Provision of draft investigative report to both parties for written response (December 30, 2031)
- Review of party responses by hearing panel

Total investigation timeline: 84 days.

**Findings**

The hearing panel considered all evidence under the [preponderance of evidence standard](https://www2.ed.gov/about/offices/list/ocr/letters/colleague-201104.pdf) (whether it is more likely than not that the alleged conduct occurred).

A central question in this matter was whether the complainant had disclosed relevant personal information to the respondent prior to the alleged incident, and whether the absence of such disclosure was material to the question of consent.

The respondent stated that he was not aware of the complainant's transgender status at the time of the alleged incident and that, had he been aware, he would not have consented to sexual activity. The panel considered whether a reasonable person in the respondent's position would have understood the complainant's prior disclosures to constitute full and explicit disclosure of the complainant's transgender status.

The panel found that the complainant's prior disclosures were ambiguous with respect to this specific information and that the respondent's account of his understanding was credible.

Given this finding, the panel determined that it was unable to assess the question of consent with the degree of certainty required under the preponderance standard, as the material facts underlying the consent analysis remained disputed.

The panel was therefore unable to reach a finding on the underlying allegation of non-consensual sexual contact.

The panel notes that an inability to reach a finding does not constitute a determination that the complainant's account is not credible.

**Sanctions**

In light of the inconclusive finding, the panel imposed the following educational measures:

- Completion of the university's online Sexual Misconduct Awareness and Prevention module (SMAP-101) within 30 days
- Written reflection assignment to be reviewed by the Office of Equity and Civil Rights Compliance

These measures are not disciplinary in nature and will not appear on the respondent's academic record.

**Appeal Rights**

Both parties have the right to appeal this determination within ten (10) business days of this notice. Grounds for appeal are limited to:

1. Procedural irregularity that affected the outcome
2. New evidence not reasonably available at the time of the hearing
3. Conflict of interest or bias on the part of a decision-maker

Appeal requests should be submitted in writing to the Dean of Students Office.

**Supportive Measures**

Regardless of outcome, the following supportive measures remain available to you:

- Student Advocacy Office: 614-555-0194
- Academic accommodations through the Dean of Students

You are not required to have further contact with the respondent. If you have concerns about contact, please notify this office.

This matter is now closed unless an appeal is submitted within the specified window.
