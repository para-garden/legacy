## COMMUNITY BENEFIT REPORT — FISCAL YEAR 2032

### A Message from Our CEO

On behalf of the 28,000 Ascend Health System team members who serve our communities every day, I am proud to present our annual Community Benefit Report.

At Ascend, our mission is clear: to provide exceptional care to every patient, regardless of ability to pay. This commitment is not merely aspirational — it is reflected in our actions. In fiscal year 2032, Ascend Health System provided **[$412 million in total community benefit](https://www.aha.org/statistics/2023-07-25-fast-facts-us-hospitals-2023)**, representing **8.4% of total operating expenses** — above the [national average of 8.1%](https://www.healthaffairs.org/doi/10.1377/hlthaff.2023.00515).

These numbers represent real people: the uninsured mother who received emergency surgery at no cost to her family. The Medicaid patient whose care was delivered at a loss. The medical residents we train who will become the next generation of healers. The community health screenings that catch disease before it becomes crisis.

We are more than a hospital system. We are a partner in the health of the communities we serve.

**Robert A. Mercer**
*President & CEO, Ascend Health System*

### Financial Assistance Program — Ascend Cares

Ascend's [financial assistance policy](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/medical-debt/) provides free or reduced-cost care to patients with household income at or below 400% of the [Federal Poverty Level](https://aspe.hhs.gov/topics/poverty-economic-mobility/poverty-guidelines). In FY2032:

| | |
|---|---|
| **Patients receiving financial assistance** | 18,400 |
| **Total charity care provided** | $70.0M |
| **Applications received** | 31,200 |
| **Applications approved** | 18,400 (59%) |
| **Applications denied — incomplete documentation** | 8,900 (29%) |
| **Applications denied — above income threshold** | 3,900 (12%) |

*Charity care is reported at gross charges per [IRS Schedule H](https://www.irs.gov/forms-pubs/about-schedule-h-form-990) guidelines.*

### Medicaid Partnership

Ascend serves as a critical safety-net provider for [Medicaid](https://www.medicaid.gov/) beneficiaries across our service area. In FY2032, Medicaid patients represented 22% of total patient volume. The gap between Medicaid reimbursement and our cost of delivering care was **$181.3 million** — a figure we absorb because access to care is not negotiable.

### Health Professions Education

Ascend sponsors [282 medical residency positions](https://www.aamc.org/data-reports/workforce/report/graduate-medical-education-financing) across 14 programs, training the physicians who will serve our communities for decades to come. Our teaching hospitals also provide clinical rotations for 840 nursing students and 1,200 allied health students annually.

**Unreimbursed cost of medical education: $61.8M**

### Our Commitment to Transparency

Ascend Health System complies with all requirements of [IRS Section 501(r)](https://www.irs.gov/charities-non-profits/financial-review-of-section-501r-requirements), including:

- Community Health Needs Assessment conducted every three years (most recent: 2031)
- Financial assistance policy publicly available in English and Spanish
- Plain-language billing summaries for all patients
- Extraordinary collection actions (ECAs) limited to accounts over 240 days past due where good-faith efforts to determine financial assistance eligibility have been exhausted

*Accounts not eligible for financial assistance and not paid within 240 days of the first post-discharge billing statement may be referred to our [third-party collections partner](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/debt-collection/) for further action.*

### Looking Ahead — FY2033 Priorities

1. Expand mobile health clinic to two additional underserved counties
2. Launch maternal health initiative addressing [maternal mortality disparities](https://www.cdc.gov/maternal-mortality/index.html) in our service area
3. Increase community health worker program from 34 to 50 CHWs
4. Partner with local schools on youth mental health screenings
5. Continue advocating for [adequate Medicaid reimbursement rates](https://www.macpac.gov/publication/medicaid-payment-policy/) at the state and federal level
