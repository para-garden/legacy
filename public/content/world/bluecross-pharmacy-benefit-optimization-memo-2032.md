### Executive Summary

Q2 performance across the Pharmacy Benefit Optimization (PBO) division exceeded targets in all key categories. Total savings attributable to formulary management, [prior authorization protocols](https://www.ama-assn.org/practice-management/prior-authorization), and step therapy requirements reached **$14.2 million** for the quarter, up from $11.8M in Q1 and representing a **20.3% year-over-year increase**.

These results reflect the continued refinement of our clinical criteria and the successful implementation of the enhanced prior authorization workflow launched in January.

### Analysis

**Prior Authorization Volume and Denial Rate**

The increase in PA requests (+18%) reflects expanded prior authorization requirements implemented in Q1 for Tier 3 and Tier 4 medications, including [GLP-1 receptor agonists](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/coverage/prescription-drug-coverage/part-d-formularies), specialty biologics, and non-preferred insulin formulations. The initial denial rate of 31.4% is above target (28%) and represents our strongest quarter since the enhanced protocol launch.

It is important to note that an initial denial is not a final determination — it is a **redirection to formulary alternatives**. In the majority of initial denials (68.6%), the prescriber either: (a) switched to the preferred formulary alternative (44.7% redirection rate), (b) submitted additional clinical documentation that resulted in approval, or (c) did not pursue an appeal.

The **44.7% formulary redirection rate** is particularly notable. This means that nearly half of all initially denied prescriptions resulted in the member receiving a formulary-preferred medication, which is both [clinically appropriate and cost-effective](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/generic-drugs/generic-drug-facts). This is the primary mechanism by which the PA process generates savings.

**Appeals**

The appeals overturn rate (32.0%) is consistent with prior quarters. Overturned appeals represent cases where additional clinical documentation demonstrated medical necessity for the non-preferred medication. This is the PA system working as designed — the initial review captures routine cases; the appeal process accommodates clinical exceptions.

[The American Medical Association's 2023 survey found that 94% of physicians reported that prior authorization delays care, and 33% reported that PA has led to a serious adverse event for a patient.](https://www.ama-assn.org/practice-management/prior-authorization/prior-authorization-data) We continue to monitor for adverse outcomes related to PA-driven formulary changes.

**Prescriber Satisfaction**

Prescriber satisfaction declined 0.3 points to 3.1/5.0, consistent with the broader industry trend as PA requirements expand. [The AMA has called for prior authorization reform](https://www.ama-assn.org/practice-management/prior-authorization), citing administrative burden on providers. We are evaluating a "gold card" pilot for high-volume prescribers with >90% PA approval rates, which would exempt qualifying prescribers from PA requirements for selected medication categories.

### Cost Avoidance Detail

| Category | Q2 Savings |
|---|---:|
| GLP-1 agonist formulary redirections | $4.8M |
| Specialty biologic step therapy | $3.9M |
| Non-preferred insulin → preferred insulin | $2.1M |
| Non-formulary generic redirections | $1.8M |
| Quantity limit enforcement | $1.6M |
| **Total** | **$14.2M** |

The GLP-1 category continues to drive the largest share of savings. [National GLP-1 spending exceeded $40 billion in 2024](https://www.cms.gov/data-research), and demand growth shows no sign of abating. Our formulary strategy — maintaining semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) as the preferred agent and requiring PA for all alternatives — has proven highly effective at managing spend in this category.

---

### Recommendations

1. **Expand PA requirements to Tier 2 specialty medications** beginning Q1 2033. Projected incremental savings: $2.4M/quarter.
2. **Implement the prescriber gold card pilot** in two regional markets (AZ, OH) starting Q4 2032. Projected impact on prescriber satisfaction: +0.4 points. Projected impact on savings: -$0.8M/quarter (acceptable trade-off for retention).
3. **Enhance member communication** on PA denials. Current denial letters reference [the right to appeal and external review](https://www.cms.gov/CCIIO/Resources/Files/external_appeals) but do not include estimated cost differentials between the denied medication and the formulary alternative. Adding this information may improve voluntary redirection rates.
4. **Conduct outcome review** on Q1 insulin formulary redirections. Ensure [HbA1c data](https://www.cdc.gov/diabetes/managing/managing-blood-sugar/a1c.html) for redirected members shows no clinically significant difference between preferred and non-preferred formulations at 6 months.
