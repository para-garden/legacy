## IMPORTANT TAX INFORMATION

Under current federal law, [loan amounts forgiven through income-driven repayment plans may be treated as taxable income](https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/p4681.pdf) in the year of forgiveness. This means that the forgiven balance may be reported to the IRS and you may owe federal (and possibly state) income taxes on the forgiven amount.

**Example based on your projected forgiveness:**

| | |
|---|---|
| Projected Forgiven Amount | ~$90,000 |
| Estimated Federal Tax Liability (22% bracket) | ~$19,800 |

> **Note:** The American Rescue Plan Act of 2021 temporarily excluded forgiven student loan amounts from taxable income through December 31, 2025. [This provision has expired.](https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/irs-student-loan-forgiveness) Forgiven amounts after 2025 are taxable unless Congress enacts new legislation. Aspire Education Services cannot provide tax advice. Please consult a qualified tax professional regarding your specific situation.
