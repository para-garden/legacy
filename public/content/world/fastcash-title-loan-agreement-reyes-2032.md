### Default and Repossession

If the borrower fails to make a scheduled payment within 30 days of the due date, the lender may:

1. **Repossess the vehicle** without further notice, pursuant to [A.R.S. § 44-281](https://www.azleg.gov/ars/44/00281.htm) and the terms of this agreement.
2. Sell the vehicle at auction to satisfy the outstanding balance.
3. Pursue the borrower for any **deficiency balance** remaining after the sale of the vehicle, plus repossession costs ($350), storage fees ($25/day), and auction fees.

*The borrower acknowledges that repossession may occur at any time and any location, including the borrower's place of residence or employment.*

*[Arizona voters rejected Proposition 200 in 2008](https://www.azsos.gov/elections), which would have extended payday lending in the state. The Deferred Presentment Companies Act expired in 2010, effectively banning payday loans. However, [motor vehicle title loans remain legal under A.R.S. § 44-281](https://www.azleg.gov/ars/44/00281.htm) with no interest rate cap. [Arizona is one of approximately 15 states that permit title lending with no rate cap.](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/what-is-a-title-loan-en-1814/)*

*[The CFPB reports that approximately 1 in 5 title loan borrowers have their vehicle repossessed.](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/research-reports/single-payment-vehicle-title-lending/) [The average title loan is $1,000, and the average borrower renews the loan 8 times](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/research-reports/single-payment-vehicle-title-lending/), paying $2,140 in interest before paying off a $1,000 loan — or losing the car. [More than half of title loan borrowers are still in debt 12 months after their first loan.](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/data-research/research-reports/single-payment-vehicle-title-lending/)*

*Carlos borrowed $1,100 against a car worth $6,200. He uses this car to drive for Uber and DoorDash. His [monthly income is approximately $2,800](https://marketplace-plan-comparison-reyes-family-2032). His [monthly expenses before this loan were $3,624](https://marketplace-plan-comparison-reyes-family-2032) — already $824 more than he earns. The title loan adds $187/month. If he misses a payment, FastCash repossesses the Camry. If FastCash repossesses the Camry, he cannot drive for Uber. If he cannot drive for Uber, he has no income. If he has no income, he cannot make the payment. The loan agreement does not describe this as a circle.*
