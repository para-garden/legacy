I need to Venmo Tyler. I feel like an asshole. I yelled at him yesterday because he unplugged my phone charger to use the outlet for his laptop, which is — I don't even care. It's an outlet. But I just snapped. Like, really snapped. He gave me this look, like, *dude, what is your problem*, and I didn't have a good answer so I just grabbed my jacket and walked out.

I should buy him a coffee or something. I'm usually not that guy.

I'm just so tired. I don't know why I can't sleep. The TV thing is normally foolproof for me — I've slept with it on in the background since I was like ten. But lately it's like my brain won't shut off unless the volume is up high enough to drown out literally everything else, and obviously I can't blast it at 3 AM with a roommate. So I just lie there. I end up scrolling on my phone or playing 2K with the sound off until my eyes burn.

I think I need to start working out again. I haven't gone to the campus rec center since I got to Whitmore. I'm getting soft. Maybe that's why I'm on edge. Lack of cardio or whatever.

Mara texted me earlier. Just a picture of Hank sleeping on the couch back home. He looks so old in the lighting in the den, all the white around his snout. I stared at the picture for like ten minutes during my macroeconomics lecture. Almost started crying in the middle of a lecture hall over a twelve-year-old beagle.

I didn't, obviously. I just sent back the heart eyes emoji. Pop another piece of Wintermint. Keep it moving.

---

Just grabbed my jacket. Gotta make sure I have gum. Okay, yep, extra pack in the right pocket. I already have a piece in, but I pop another one anyway.

The room is so quiet right now. Tyler must still be at his bio lab. I hate being in here when it's dead silent. The mini-fridge makes this weird clicking noise that you don't hear until everything else stops, and once you hear it, it just drills into your head.

I pulled my phone out to text the floor chat, but I'm just staring at the screen.

*Dining hall in 10?* That's all I have to type.

Just hit send, Evan.

Someone out in the hall just laughed, really loud, and I actually flinched. Like, physically jerked my shoulder. What is wrong with me? It's just Dave or Marcus or somebody goofing off by the elevators. But for a split second my brain went right back to the basement at that off-campus house a couple weeks ago. It was so loud down there. The music was literally vibrating in my teeth. It was so hot, and it smelled like spilled vodka and sweat and —

Nope. Not doing that. It was just a weird night. Parties get sloppy. It's college. I went home and played *Halo* for three hours after, and it was fine. I'm fine.

I just need to eat. I haven't really eaten today except a bagel at like nine. No wonder I'm feeling kind of shaky. That's why I'm on edge.

*Dining hall in 10?* Send.

Okay. Good. Marcus already replied. *Bet. Meet u by the stairs.*

I'm just going to go down there, get a burger, talk about the Bulls' defensive rotation, and just chill out. Be the easy guy. I'm always the easy guy. It's not hard.

I just really wish my chest would stop feeling so tight. Must be the caffeine from the energy drink I had before class.

---

Marcus is waiting by the stairwell when I get out there. He's wearing his slides with socks, complaining about his history TA. I hit him with a fist bump, and he makes some joke about how I look like I just woke up, and I let out this massive laugh. It echoes down the cinderblock hallway. It feels good to hear it. Like, *see? I'm here. We're good.*

We walk across the quad to the dining hall. The air is actually getting cold now, that real Illinois October cold that bites at your ears. It usually wakes me up. But my legs feel kind of heavy.

The dining hall is packed. A wall of noise when you walk in. Plates clattering, people shouting across tables, the machines humming. Usually I love this. Today, the fluorescent lights just seem really, really bright. It's making the back of my eyes throb.

We get our food and find a table near the back. I load up my tray — burger, fries, a huge pile of pasta. I told myself I was starving. But now I'm looking down at the grease soaking into the paper boat holding the fries, and my stomach does this slow, hard flip.

I take a bite of the burger anyway. It tastes like ash, but I force it down.

"So," Marcus says, shaking some hot sauce onto his chicken. "Dave was saying those guys on Elm Street are throwing another thing this Saturday. The basement setup. You down? We didn't really get to stay long last time."

I stop chewing.

The dining hall is still loud. Everything is still happening. But it feels like all the sound just sucked right out of my ears. My heart gives this one massive, stupid thump against my ribs, so hard I swear Marcus should be able to see it through my hoodie.

"Nah," I say. My voice sounds totally normal. How does my voice sound normal? "I don't know, man. That house is kind of grimy."

"Bro, all the houses are grimy," Marcus laughs. "But okay, Mr. High Standards. We can just hit the dorm parties."

"Yeah. Yeah, dorms are better." I take a sip of my Coke. The ice hits my teeth. "Plus the Bulls are playing Cleveland on Saturday. We gotta see if they can actually guard Mitchell. Have you looked at the defensive rating without..."

I just start talking. I talk about the pick-and-roll coverage. I talk about the bench rotation. I go on for like five straight minutes. Marcus is nodding, throwing in a comment here and there, just eating his food. He has no idea. He thinks we're just talking basketball.

Under the table, my leg is bouncing so fast my heel is numb.

---

I run out of things to say about the Bulls. Marcus is looking at his phone now, half-listening.

Someone bumps the back of my chair.

It's just a guy trying to squeeze past our table with a tray, but my whole body flinches. My knee slams up into the underside of the table. The water glasses rattle. Marcus looks up.

"You good, man?"

"Yeah," I say. I swallow hard. The gum goes right down my throat. I don't even care. "Yeah, someone just kicked my chair."

"Trays are too damn big," Marcus says, going back to his phone.

I need to leave. I can't be in here anymore. The smell of the hot sauce and the grease is making the back of my throat water. The lights are buzzing. I swear I can hear them buzzing over the sound of two hundred people talking.

"Hey, I just remembered," I say, and I'm already standing up. "I gotta buy Tyler that Gatorade. Before the market closes."

Marcus looks at the clock on the wall. "Market's open till midnight, bro."

"Yeah, but I just want to get it done. Catch you back at the dorm?"

"Alright. Hit the chat when the game starts."

I dump my tray at the dish return so fast I almost drop a plate. I push through the double doors and out onto the quad, and the chill hits my face, and I take this massive, greedy breath.

*Okay.*

*Okay, see? You're fine.*

Just needed some air. It was stuffy in there. Anyone would get a headache.

I start walking back toward the dorms. I find the fresh pack of gum, peel the wrapper off a piece with my thumb, and pop it in. Wintermint. The sharp taste kind of centers me a little.

I don't go to the market. I just want to get to my bed. I just want to turn the TV on.

---

I unlock my door. Tyler is at his desk, wearing his big noise-canceling headphones.

I shut the door, maybe a little harder than I mean to, and he flinches, sliding one of the earcups back.

"Hey," he says. His voice is kind of flat.

"Hey. Look, man. My bad about yesterday. With the charger. I was just totally burnt out from studying for econ."

It's an easy lie. People always accept 'studying' as an excuse for being an asshole.

Tyler looks at me for a second. "It's whatever," he says. Then he kind of squints at me. "You good? You look like you're gonna pass out."

I glance at the cheap mirror stuck to the back of our door. I do look terrible. Dark, purplish smudges under my eyes. My skin has this weird grayish tint to it.

"Yeah, I'm good," I say, forcing a laugh. "Think the dining hall burger is fighting back. I'm just gonna crash."

Relief washes over me when he grabs his backpack for the library. Like, physical, knee-weakening relief. I can just have the room.

The door clicks shut behind him.

I don't even change into sweatpants. I kick my sneakers off and grab the remote. ESPN. *SportsCenter*. Volume up.

20. 25.

It's not loud enough. I can still hear my own breathing. I can still feel the blood rushing in my ears.

35.

That's better. Scott Van Pelt's voice filling up the cinderblock room. Stats. Names. Numbers. Safe.

I collapse onto my mattress. I'm still chewing the wintermint gum. My jaw needs something to do.

*Just watch the screen, Evan. Just listen to the numbers.*

But my chest is so tight. I try to take a deep breath, but it catches halfway down.

I close my eyes, but the second I do, it's dark, and the TV noise isn't *SportsCenter* anymore, it's bass coming through the floorboards, and the heat in my face isn't from the dorm radiator, it's from the basement, and —

I snap my eyes open.

"Nope," I say out loud. To the empty room. "Nope."

Volume up. 45.

It's deafening now. Scott Van Pelt is basically shouting at me about a backup quarterback in Carolina.

I just stare at the screen. My eyes are burning so bad. I'm so exhausted I feel nauseous. I just want to sleep. Why can't I just sleep? I always sleep.

---

*Thump, thump, thump.* The drywall right next to my head vibrates.

"Hey! Turn it down!"

I scramble up, fumbling around the mattress for the remote. 30. 20. 12.

The silence crashes back into the room. It feels heavier this time.

"Sorry!" I yell toward the wall. My voice cracks. "My bad!"

I'm sitting on the edge of my bed now. Breathing like I just ran stadium stairs. Short, sharp gasps that don't actually fill my lungs. My hands are shaking. Visibly trembling. I watch my own fingers for a second, completely detached from them, wondering why they're doing that.

I can't do the TV anymore. The one thing that has always, always worked, doesn't work.

I grab my phone. I open the picture of Hank.

I just look at him. Curled up on the nice couch cushions, snoring his little beagle snore. Safe in the den in Naperville. I zoom in on his face, the white fur around his nose. I remember sitting on the floor with him when he got sick junior year, letting him rest his heavy head on my knee, thinking *please don't go, please don't go.* It was scary, but it was simple. I knew exactly what I was scared of. Everybody else knew what I was scared of, too.

A drop of water hits the phone screen. It magnifies the pixels in Hank's ear.

I blink, and another drop falls.

I reach up and touch my face. It's totally wet.

I wipe my eyes with the heel of my hand, pressing hard until I see spots. It's just screen fatigue. The blue light is bad for your eyes. I've just been staring at screens too much. I'm just out of shape, and I had too much caffeine, and the dining hall was too bright, and my eyes are tired.

That's all it is.

I lock the phone and put it face down. I lie back on the mattress, in the quiet room, and pull the blanket all the way up over my head, and I just wait for the morning.

---

It gets hot under the comforter fast. I'm just breathing my own air.

I squeeze my eyes shut. I don't picture the basement. I don't picture the heavy wooden door, or the heat, or the way the music vibrated in my teeth. I don't. I push it away, same as I push away a bad play on the field. You just drop it and move on to the next one.

Tomorrow is Wednesday. I have a 9 AM communications seminar.

I'll get up early. I'll go to the campus rec center. I'll get on a treadmill and run until my lungs burn. I'll sweat out whatever this weird bug is, or this caffeine crash, or this slump. I just need to get the toxins out.

I'll buy Tyler the Gatorade. I'll text the floor chat and see who wants to grab lunch. I'll be Evan. I'm the easy guy. I'm the one nobody has to worry about.

I just have to lie here until it's tomorrow. That's all. You just close your eyes, and you lie here, and eventually the sun comes up and it's a new day and you just go back to normal.

I'm fine. I'm completely fine.
