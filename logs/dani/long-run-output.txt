She navigates through a family server, tapping through folders that have been migrated, copied, and preserved across a hundred years of changing operating systems. She finds a folder titled *Field Guide for Sofia*. 

`` `Energy cannot be created or destroyed. It only changes forms. I am the carbon in the buttonbush. I am the code on the screen.` ``

She opens the oldest file in the archive. 

The screen glows in the twilight. She traces her short, capable thumb over the words written by a twenty-one-year-old girl who thought the world had ended in a cinderblock basement, a lifetime ago. 

She reads the data. She reads the methodology of how to build a retaining wall. She reads how the chickadee goes up, and the nuthatch goes down. 

A red-winged blackbird calls out from the reeds. The young woman lowers the tablet, looking out at the massive, thriving canopy of the climax community. She takes a deep, effortless breath of the clean Ohio air. 

`` `The experiment is complete. The variables are stable. The mud is safe to touch.` ``

She taps the screen. The document closes.
